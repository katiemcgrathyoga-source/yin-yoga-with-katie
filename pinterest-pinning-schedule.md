# Pinterest Pinning Schedule — Yin Yoga with Katie

A sustainable, batch-friendly pinning routine that turns the pins your site already
generates (`/pins`) into steady Pinterest traffic — Katie's priority distribution
channel. Built for a part-time schedule: **make a week of pins in one sitting,
schedule them, done.**

Profile: https://www.pinterest.com/YinYogaWithKatie

---

## The principle

**Consistency beats volume, and fresh pins beat repeats.** Pinning ~2 quality pins
a day, every day, does far more than 20 pins once a week. And Pinterest rewards
*fresh* images — so the goal isn't to re-pin the same graphic; it's to make a *new*
pin design pointing at the same page. Your `/pins` generator is built for exactly
this: it produces several templates per item (band, numbered, checklist, quote,
ritual, photo, text), so one pose or post becomes many distinct pins over time.

**Target: ~2 fresh pins/day (14/week).** Sustainable, and plenty to grow.

## Boards to run

Create these if they don't exist yet — a handful of clear, keyword-named boards:

1. **Yoga for Runners** ← the current push (new)
2. **Yin Yoga for Beginners**
3. **Yin Yoga for Sleep & Relaxation**
4. **Yin Yoga Poses & Cues** (the pose library)
5. **Yin Yoga Routines & Sequences**
6. **Yoga for Flexibility & Mobility**
7. **Yin Yoga for Stress & Calm**

Board names are search terms — keep them plain and descriptive, and put a real
keyword sentence in each board description.

## The weekly rhythm

**Sunday (≈30–40 min): batch.** Open `/pins`, generate next week's pins across your
content, and load them into your scheduler (Pinterest's native scheduler, or the
copy-for-scheduler output). Then you don't touch it again all week.

**Mon–Sat: 2 scheduled pins go out per day** (below). Vary the board and the pin
template so nothing repeats.

| Day | Pin 1 | Pin 2 | Boards |
|-----|-------|-------|--------|
| **Mon** | A pose guide | A routine | Poses · Routines |
| **Tue** | A blog post | A pose guide | (topic board) · Poses |
| **Wed** | A routine | A pose guide | Routines · Flexibility |
| **Thu** | **Runner content** | A pose guide | Yoga for Runners · Poses |
| **Fri** | A pose guide | A routine | Poses · Sleep or Calm |
| **Sat** | A blog post | A quote/ritual pin | (topic board) · Beginners |

Rotate *which* pose/routine/post each week, and rotate the *template* so the same
URL always gets a fresh-looking pin. A single pose page can supply a photo pin one
week, a cues/checklist pin a few weeks later, and a quote pin after that.

## The 6-week Runner campaign (do this now)

While you build the runner funnel, weight Thursdays (and one extra pin midweek)
toward the **Yoga for Runners** board:

- Pin the new **[Yoga for Runners blog](https://yinyogawithkatie.com/blog/yoga-for-runners)** in 3–4 different templates over the six weeks.
- Pin the **free Post-Run Reset** landing page (`/runners`) — this is the one that
  captures emails. Give it a clear "Free 10-minute routine for runners" pin.
- Pin the runner-relevant **pose guides** (Dragon, Sleeping Swan, Caterpillar,
  Toe Squat, Twisted Dragon) to the Yoga for Runners board with a "for runners" angle.
- Pin a couple of **routines** (deep-legs, lower-back) framed for runners.

Goal: get the Yoga for Runners board to ~20–30 quality pins so it starts ranking
and feeding `/runners`.

## What to pin from where

- **Pose pages** — your deepest well (42 of them). Photo pins, cue/checklist pins,
  "how long to hold" pins. This is the everyday backbone.
- **Routines** — "20-min Yin for X" pins; strong savers.
- **Blog posts** — the highest-intent pins (they lead to a page with a signup).
  Pin each post several times in different templates across its life.
- **Landing pages** — `/runners` (and `/free-class`) as the conversion pins.
- **Seasonal/quote pins** — a calm quote or a "ritual" pin for variety and reach.

---

## Rules of thumb

- **Always link to your own site**, never straight to YouTube — a pin should land on
  a page that can capture an email (pose, routine, blog, or `/runners`).
- **Fresh image every time.** New template or new photo to the same URL; don't
  re-upload an identical pin.
- **Write the pin description like a person searching** — "yin yoga for tight hips
  after running," not "hip pin 3." Include the keyword.
- **Spread across boards** — don't dump five pins to one board in a day.
- **Don't chase volume.** Two thoughtful pins a day, every day, is the whole game.

---

## What to watch (monthly, 5 min)

In Pinterest analytics, note which **pins and boards** drive the most outbound
clicks to the site — then make more pins like the winners, on the winning boards.
Pair this with the site's `signup_source` data: if Pinterest is sending runners who
sign up at `/runners`, lean harder into the Runner campaign.
