# The Runner's Reset — Course Production Plan

The 12 follow-along sessions to film, as full Yin sequences — built from your
existing pose library, anchored to the tissues running actually tightens, and
kept honest on the science. **Two sessions per week over six weeks: a short
Post-Run Reset and a longer Rest-Day Deep Stretch.** (No pre-run work — long
passive Yin holds before a run can blunt performance; Yin belongs after a run
and on rest days.)

---

## How to talk about the benefits (stay credible)

**What Yin can honestly claim for runners:**
- **Improved range of motion / flexibility** — long-duration static holds reliably
  increase tissue extensibility and joint ROM (hips, hamstrings, ankles).
- **Restored ankle & hip mobility** — which matters for running mechanics.
- **Recovery & relaxation** — the long, quiet holds shift you toward the
  parasympathetic (rest) state; runners report feeling less stiff and more rested.

**What NOT to claim** (or you'll lose a sharp audience):
- Don't say Yin "flushes lactic acid / toxins" — that's a myth.
- Don't say you "lengthen the IT band" — it's a tough tendon; you address the
  **muscles that feed it** (TFL, glutes) and the lateral line. Frame it that way.
- Keep it "helps with / eases / improves," not "cures / prevents injury." Pair the
  course with your usual physician disclaimer.

## Yin principles (say these in the intros)

- **Holds:** rest-day 3–5 min · post-run 2–3 min (muscles are warm).
- **The edge:** go to about **80%**, never force. There's no right or wrong shape.
- **Sharp or nervy pain = back off.** Dull stretch is fine; electric/nerve pain isn't.
- **Props:** a bolster, two blocks, a blanket, a strap. Everything has a smaller option.
- **Not for acute injury** — general education, not medical advice.

## The runner's tight-tissue map (the science backbone)

| Running tightens… | Because | Yin poses that target it |
|---|---|---|
| **Hip flexors** (psoas, iliacus, rectus femoris) | repetitive hip flexion + sitting → limits hip extension | Dragon, Saddle, Sphinx/Seal, Supported Bridge |
| **Hamstrings** | high eccentric load; common strain site | Caterpillar, Half-Butterfly, Dragonfly, Reclined Hamstring |
| **Glutes & piriformis** | stabilizers; tightness → lateral hip/knee niggles | Sleeping Swan, Shoelace, Deer, Reclined Swan, Thread-the-Needle |
| **Calves, ankles, feet** | huge load; stiffness cuts ankle dorsiflexion | Toe-Squat, Ankle-Stretch, Squat, Dangling |
| **Quads** | eccentric load (esp. downhill) | Saddle, Twisted Dragon |
| **Outer hip / TFL / "IT band"** | lateral stabiliser overload | Twisted Dragon, Swan, Banana, Shoelace |
| **Adductors / groin** | stride mechanics | Butterfly, Dragonfly, Frog, Happy Baby |
| **Lower back & spine** | compression + anterior pelvic tilt | Sphinx/Seal, Caterpillar, Reclined Twist, Banana, Child's, Supported Bridge |
| **Whole system** | needs to down-shift to recover | Legs Up the Wall, Corpse, supported poses |

## The 12 sessions

Each week: a short **Post-Run Reset** (do it right after a run, while you're warm)
and a longer **Rest-Day Deep Stretch** (the weekly reset). *(R/L = hold each side.)*

### Week 1 — Hips & hip flexors

**S1 · Post-Run Hip Reset — ~12 min (2-min holds)**
Constructive rest 1 min → Sphinx 2 → Dragon R/L 2 each → Sleeping Swan R/L 2 each → Reclined Twist R/L 1 each.
*Why: opens the hip flexion/extension the run just loaded, plus a glute release for lateral balance.*

**S2 · Rest-Day Deep Hips — ~38 min (3–5-min holds)**
Constructive rest 2 → Butterfly 4 → Half-Butterfly R/L 3 each → Dragon R/L 4 each → Sleeping Swan R/L 4 each → Reclined Twist R/L 2 each → Legs Up the Wall 4. *(Add Shoelace R/L 3 each if time.)*
*Why: the deepest work into the hip capsule, flexors and glutes — the runner's tightest region.*

### Week 2 — Hamstrings & glutes

**S3 · Post-Run Hamstring & Glute Flush — ~12 min**
Constructive rest 1 → Caterpillar 2 → Half-Butterfly R/L 2 each → Sleeping Swan R/L 2 each → Reclined Hamstring R/L 1 each.
*Why: eases the eccentric-loaded hamstrings and glutes while they're warm.*

**S4 · Rest-Day Deep Hams & Glutes — ~38 min**
Constructive rest 2 → Caterpillar 5 → Half-Butterfly R/L 4 each → Dragonfly 4 → Sleeping Swan R/L 4 each → Shoelace R/L 3 each → Corpse 3.
*Why: the full posterior chain — the tissue most prone to runner's strains.*

### Week 3 — Calves, ankles & feet

**S5 · Post-Run Feet & Calf Reset — ~12 min (shorter holds — feet tolerate less)**
Toe-Squat 1–2 → Ankle-Stretch 1–2 → Dangling 2 → Squat (Malasana) 2 → half-Saddle 2 → seated rest 1.
*Why: restores the ankle and foot mobility that pounding stiffens.*

**S6 · Rest-Day Head-to-Toe Legs — ~35 min**
Constructive rest 2 → Toe-Squat 2 → Ankle-Stretch 2 → Squat 3 → Dragon R/L 3 each → Half-Butterfly R/L 3 each → Saddle 4 → Dangling 2 → Legs Up the Wall 4.
*Why: the whole lower chain from the feet up — the base runners always skip.*

### Week 4 — Quads & the outer hip (the "IT band" story, done right)

**S7 · Post-Run Quad & Outer-Hip Quick — ~12 min**
Constructive rest 1 → half-Saddle R/L 2 each → Twisted Dragon R/L 2 each → Banana R/L 2 each.
*Why: eases the quads and the lateral-hip line the run just loaded.*

**S8 · Rest-Day Quads, Outer Hip & Lateral Line — ~38 min**
Constructive rest 2 → Sphinx 3 → Saddle 5 → Dragon→Twisted Dragon R 3+3, L 3+3 → Swan R/L 3 each → Banana R/L 3 each → Corpse 3.
*Why: works the TFL, glutes and lateral line behind most "IT band" complaints — the band itself doesn't stretch; the muscles feeding it do.*

### Week 5 — Lower back & spine

**S9 · Post-Run Low-Back Release — ~12 min**
Constructive rest 1 → knees-to-chest / Wind-Relieving 2 → Sphinx 2 → Child's Pose 2 → Reclined Twist R/L 2 each → rest 1.
*Why: mobilises the spine in every direction after the compression of the run.*

**S10 · Rest-Day Back & Posterior Chain — ~38 min**
Constructive rest 2 → Sphinx 3 → Seal 3 → Child's Pose 3 → Caterpillar 5 → Supported Bridge 4 → Banana R/L 3 each → Reclined Twist R/L 3 each → Legs Up the Wall 4.
*Why: undoes the compression and forward pelvic tilt of every run — gentle flexion, extension, rotation, side-bend.*

### Week 6 — The full lower body, integrated

**S11 · Post-Run Full Lower-Body Flush — ~15 min** *(your everyday go-to)*
Constructive rest 1 → Dragon R/L 2 each → Sleeping Swan R/L 2 each → Caterpillar 2 → Reclined Twist R/L 1 each.
*Why: one short session that touches the whole chain — the one you'll reach for most.*

**S12 · Rest-Day The Long Reset — ~46 min**
Constructive rest 3 → Butterfly 4 → Half-Butterfly R/L 3 each → Dragon R/L 3 each → Sleeping Swan R/L 4 each → Saddle 4 → Caterpillar 4 → Reclined Twist R/L 2 each → Legs Up the Wall 4 → Corpse 3.
*Why: the full recovery ritual — every link in one flowing sequence.*

## Safety flags to cue on camera

- **Saddle / half-Saddle** — intense on knees, quads, low back. Always show the
  propped version (bolster behind), knees together, come out slowly. Offer Sphinx
  as the easy alternative.
- **Toe-Squat** — sharp for tight feet. 30–60s is plenty at first; hands down to
  take weight off.
- **Frog / deep Dragonfly** — go shallow; knee comfort first.
- Repeat every session: **80%, no forcing, sharp/nervy pain means back off.**

## Filming notes

- **One video = one session (12 total).** Film the six **Rest-Day** deep sessions
  first — they contain nearly every pose; the six Post-Run shorts are subsets you
  can film in the same block.
- **Batch by pose** if easier, then edit into sessions — you hold each shape anyway.
- Open each with your normal welcome + the 80%/no-forcing cue; close with your
  "take good care of yourself." Put the hold time on screen (you have the timer styling).
- Keep props identical across videos (bolster, 2 blocks, strap) so viewers aren't
  hunting for gear.
- These 12 are the **paid** films (unlisted/members). Any offcuts make great free
  runner Shorts and YouTube videos — which feed the funnel. One filming block, two
  jobs done.
