# Repurpose a blog post into an email — Yin Yoga with Katie

The lightweight ongoing rhythm that keeps the list warm *between* the welcome
sequence and any launch. Every time you publish a Journal post, turn it into one
short broadcast. Aim for five minutes — you're reusing the hook, quote and FAQ
you already wrote, not writing from scratch.

Written in Katie's real voice: warm, plain, gentle, permission-giving. No hype,
no pet names, no big claims. Short sentences. Sign-off is always
*"take good care of yourself."*

---

## This is a Campaign, NOT the welcome automation

Keep the two mechanisms separate:

| | **Welcome automation** | **Repurpose broadcast (this)** |
|---|---|---|
| Trigger | Someone signs up | You hit send, when a post goes live |
| Timing | Relative to *their* signup (day 2, 4, 8) | Relative to *the calendar* |
| Who gets it | Each new subscriber, once | Your whole list (or a segment), now |
| Cadence | Fixed, set-and-forget (leave it at 4) | Every 1–2 weeks, manual |

**Do not add these to the welcome automation.** If you did, the post would only
reach *future* joiners on a delay, and your existing list — the people you most
want to keep warm — would never see it.

### In MailerLite
1. **Campaigns → Create campaign → Regular campaign.**
2. Send to your **main subscriber group** (later, you can segment by
   `signup_source` if a post is topic-specific — e.g. only sleep signups).
3. One post → one email → one primary link. Send, done.

---

## The format — six slots

1. **Subject** — the reader's *feeling or problem*, not "new blog post." Pull it
   from the post's hook or its `pin_quote`.
2. **Preview line** — one supporting sentence.
3. **Warm open** — one human line (seasonal, or a feeling). One sentence.
4. **The one idea** — the single most useful takeaway from the post, 2–4 short
   sentences. *Not* the whole post. (An FAQ answer often works perfectly here.)
5. **Bridge + link** — "I wrote the rest down for you →" to the post, and/or a
   second soft link straight to its practise-along routine or video.
6. **Sign-off** — *take good care of yourself, Katie.*

### Rules that keep it 5 minutes and on-brand
- One idea, one primary link. Never a link dump.
- Lead with how they *feel*, not with your publishing schedule.
- 150–250 words. 2–3 short paragraphs. Skimmable.
- Reuse what's already in the post: the pull-quote becomes the subject; an FAQ
  answer becomes "the one idea"; the practise-along block becomes the second link.
- No forcing, no "you must." Everything is an invitation.

## Worked example (bedtime post)

> **Subject:** for the nights you can't switch off
> **Preview:** a gentler way into sleep — no forcing.
>
> Hi {$name|default:"there"},
>
> If you've been lying awake lately with a busy head, this one's for you.
>
> The thing I keep coming back to: you can't *make* yourself fall asleep — the
> harder you try, the further off it gets. What you *can* do is give your body a
> soft, slow signal that the day is over. That's really all bedtime Yin is. A few
> quiet shapes, held long enough for your nervous system to catch up.
>
> I wrote down the why, and a short sequence you can do in bed tonight:
>
> **▶ [Read it here](https://yinyogawithkatie.com/blog/…)** — or just
> **[press play on the wind-down](https://yinyogawithkatie.com/routines/bedtime-wind-down)**.
>
> Take good care of yourself,
> Katie

## Publish-day checklist
- [ ] Post is live on the site
- [ ] Subject = the feeling/hook (or the post's `pin_quote`)
- [ ] One idea lifted from the post (often an FAQ answer)
- [ ] One primary link (the post), optional second link (practise-along)
- [ ] Under ~250 words, signed off *take good care of yourself*
- [ ] Send as a **Campaign** to the main group
- [ ] (You're already pinning the post — see the pin console at `/pins`.)
