## What this Yin class is

A gentle 30-minute full-body Yin practice, made with complete beginners in mind. We move slowly through just four poses — Reclined Swan for the hips and glutes, Half-Butterfly for the hamstrings and lower back, Supported Fish to open the chest, and a Reclined Twist for the spine — before settling into a final rest. Each shape is held long and slow, so you can stop reaching and let the pose do the work. Come as you are; there's a lighter option and a deeper one everywhere, and you're always welcome to choose the one your body wants today.

## Who this class is for

Anyone new to Yin, or anyone who just wants to slow right down and unwind. If you'd like to learn how to be still, follow your breath, and feel your way through a pose rather than force it, you're in exactly the right place. It's a lovely practice to return to after a long day, or any time your body is asking for a little space. There's no rush and nothing to get right — just four spacious holds and permission to soften.

## What you need

Your mat, and one block to hand — a firm cushion or a folded blanket works just as well. You might slip it under a knee in Half-Butterfly, and you'll rest back over it in Supported Fish, so having a second block or cushion nearby for your head is a nice touch if you'd like more support. Beyond that, all you need is your breath and a warm, quiet corner where you can be still. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Ease only to that first point of resistance — the first sensation you feel — and stay there. No pulling, no forcing; if you're feeling something, you're doing the pose.
- **Hold for time.** We stay in each pose for around three minutes so the shape can gently release. If the sensation fades, you're welcome to soften a little deeper; come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Once you're settled, let the fidgeting go and rest your attention on your breath and on the sensations in your body. This is where the practice quietly does its work.
