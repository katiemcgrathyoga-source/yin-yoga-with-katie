## What this Yin class is

A gentle 20-minute morning Yin practice — a slow, full-body stretch to help you wake up without rushing. We begin lying down in a reclined butterfly, letting the breath get longer and softer, then drift through easy hip openers in reclined swan and a supported twist. From there we come up to sit for eagle arms, a little neck and shoulder release, and a soft seated twist to finish. No props needed, and every pose has a lighter option and a deeper one — so you can meet your morning body exactly where it is.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who likes to ease into the day slowly rather than jumping straight into something strong. It's lovely if you wake up feeling a little stiff through the hips, lower back, or shoulders, and it's completely beginner-friendly — nothing here forces or strains. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace and let the shapes do the work.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet corner where you can be still for twenty minutes. No props required — though if sitting cross-legged feels tight, a cushion or folded blanket under the hips is always welcome. Press pause whenever you need to, and stay as long as you like in the closing rest.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing, and especially gentle first thing in the morning.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; let the shape do the work rather than pushing into it. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — letting each inhale loosen a little tension and each exhale carry it away.
