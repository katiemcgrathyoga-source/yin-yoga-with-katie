## What this Yin class is

A full hour of silent Yin — a simple full-body stretch with minimal cues and no props needed. I name each pose and how long we'll stay, then let you sink into the quiet, so you can drop out of your head and fully into the shape. We move gently from the back and shoulders into the hips and legs, holding each pose long and slow to let deep tension unwind. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who loves a peaceful, meditative practice with very little talking — before bed, after a long day, or any time you need to shut the world out and settle. It's also lovely if you want a deep, unhurried stretch without gathering any props. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props required — though a cushion or blanket nearby is always welcome. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; let the shape do the work. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — especially helpful in a silent practice.
