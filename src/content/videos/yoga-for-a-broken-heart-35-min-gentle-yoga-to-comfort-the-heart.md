## What this Yin class is

A gentle 35 minutes made for the days when the heart feels heavy. We begin seated with some soft, calming breathing, then settle onto our backs and open slowly through the chest and hips in a reclined butterfly. From there we fold into child's pose, melt the upper back open in melting heart, ease out the neck, and close with a little stillness. It's slow, cosy and undemanding from start to finish — a quiet place to land and breathe.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants a soft, comforting practice to move through when they're feeling tender, low or simply in need of some gentleness. It suits all levels, whether you're brand new to Yin or well practised — nothing here is strong or strenuous. If you'd like company and a calm voice to guide you, this is for you.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. A pillow or cushion nearby is lovely — to hold close, to rest your forehead on, or to sit up on — and a couple of blocks work well too if you have them. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, so take whichever your body wants today and pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
