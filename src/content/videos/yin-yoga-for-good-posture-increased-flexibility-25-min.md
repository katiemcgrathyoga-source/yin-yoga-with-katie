## What this Yin class is

A gentle 25-minute Yin practice to open the chest and shoulders and ease the way sitting closes us down through the day. We hold each pose for two to four minutes, letting the deeper connective tissues slowly release rather than muscling into a stretch. We begin softly in extended child's pose, melt the heart toward the mat, open the shoulders with cow-face arms and a strap, then drape over a block in supported fish and rest on the blocks in a supported bridge — finishing in a long, quiet savasana. Come as you are; every pose has a lighter option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who spends a lot of the day hunched over a desk, a wheel, or a phone and wants to feel a little more open across the chest and shoulders. It's a kind, unhurried practice — no strength or bendiness required — so it suits beginners as well as anyone craving a slow upper-body stretch. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace and let a strap and a block do some of the work for you.

## What you need

Your mat, a strap or belt, and one or two yoga blocks — a couple of thick books will stand in for the blocks just fine. A cushion or folded blanket nearby is always welcome for a little extra padding under the head, chest, or hips. Find a warm, quiet spot where you can be still, and press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real, honest stretch, but never forcing or pushing into pain.
- **Hold for time.** These poses are held a few minutes; soften and let the shape do the work rather than gripping. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath and the sensations in your body — when the mind wanders, gently guide it back.
