## What this Yin class is

A full 70 minutes of silent Yin — a slow, full-body stretch with minimal cues to enjoy meditative bliss. This is Class 6 of the Yin Yoga Challenge. I say just enough to guide you into each shape and tell you how long we'll stay, offer a prop or an alternative, then leave you to the quiet so you can sink in and let the pose do its work. Long, unhurried holds open the hips, release the back and shoulders, and unwind a twist through the spine. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who loves a long, peaceful practice with very little talking — a chance to sit with yourself, notice your thoughts, and simply observe rather than engage. It's a beautiful way to relieve stress and tension and settle a busy mind. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. No props required — though a cushion, bolster or blanket nearby is always welcome to soften the reclined shapes. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a few minutes; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — especially helpful in a silent practice.
