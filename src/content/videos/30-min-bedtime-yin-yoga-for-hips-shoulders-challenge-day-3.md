## What this Yin class is

A gentle half-hour of Yin to close the day, with a focus on your hips and shoulders — the places we tend to hold the most after hours of sitting and hunching over screens. We settle into Dragon to open the hips and hip flexors, fold forward from an easy cross-legged seat to reach into the glutes and lower back, then open the shoulders and chest with Cow-Face arms before a soft spinal twist. Each shape is held for a few slow minutes. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, and you choose what feels right tonight.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to unwind before bed, whether you're brand new to Yin or already have a practice. If your shoulders have been curling forward all day, or your hips feel tight and restless, this class meets you where you are. It's Day 3 of the Bedtime Challenge, but it stands on its own too — dip in whenever you need to switch off.

## What you need

Just your mat, and somewhere warm and quiet where you can be still. A strap or belt is handy for the Cow-Face arms, and a couple of yoga blocks are nice for the hips and to sit on — but none of it is essential, and I'll show you how to do every pose without props. A cushion or blanket nearby is lovely for extra comfort. Press pause any time you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. Softer is perfect before bed.
- **Hold for time.** Stay a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Let the body and mind settle, and follow your breath as it slows.
