## What this Yin class is

A fully reclined, silent Yin class where a single bolster does all the hard work, so all you have to do is soften and sink. We stay lying down the whole way through — opening gently through the hips in reclined butterfly, resting with the bolster under the knees, easing into reclined swan and a slow twist, floating in a supported happy baby, then opening the chest over the bolster in supported fish before melting into corpse. With minimal cues and long, quiet holds, it's a class to feel rather than think through. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to lie down, be held by a bolster and let the day drain away. It's ideal in the evening or before bed, and because everything is supported and reclined, it suits complete beginners just as much as seasoned practitioners. If you find a chatty class distracting, the quiet, minimal cueing here gives your mind room to settle.

## What you need

A bolster is all you really need — or a firm cushion or a couple of folded blankets if you don't have one. A blanket over you for warmth is lovely, as we stay still for a good while, and a quiet, dimly lit space helps you drop in. Press pause whenever you need to, and remember every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
