## What this Yin class is

Forty minutes of Yin that works its way right up the body — we start at the toes and ankles, travel through the hamstrings, hips and lower back, and finish in the shoulders, wrists and neck. Because it's Yin, we hold each shape a good while — around one to three minutes — so the stretch has time to reach past the muscles and into the deeper connective tissue around your joints. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone craving a proper, top-to-toe deep stretch, and anyone who sits or stands a lot and feels the stiffness settle in the feet, hamstrings and shoulders. It's an all-levels class — a few poses, like the toe squat and reverse prayer, are wonderfully intense, but there's always a way to make them yours. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props are required — though a yoga block or cushion is lovely to sit on or to lift the hips, and a folded blanket is handy to pad the knees when we take them to the mat. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. We won't tolerate any pain.
- **Hold for time.** Stay a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Come out sooner whenever your body asks — you don't need my permission.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, soften the jaw and shoulders, and rest your attention on your breath.
