## What this Yin class is

A full-body deep stretch, held long and slow with no props at all. We start up in the shoulders, work down through the spine, and open into the hips and legs — so by the end you've stretched away tension from head to toe. Each pose is held for around five minutes, because in Yin it's the length of the hold that does the work. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants a proper, unhurried full-body stretch and a deep sense of relaxation — after a long day, before bed, or any time your body feels tight and your mind feels busy. Five-minute holds are wonderfully calming, but if that ever feels too long, come out sooner. Always respect your own body and your own limitations. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. This is a no-props class, so there's nothing to gather — simply press play. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** We stay around five minutes in each shape; let the shape do the work, and come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath.
