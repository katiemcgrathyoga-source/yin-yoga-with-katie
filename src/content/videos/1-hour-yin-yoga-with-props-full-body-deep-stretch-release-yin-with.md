## What this Yin class is

A full hour of Yin with a bolster and a couple of blocks, so the deeper shapes feel restful rather than effortful. We hold each pose for around three minutes and let the props do the holding — which means you can soften completely and let the stretch reach a little deeper. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who loves feeling fully supported, or who finds unpropped Yin a bit intense — and anyone who simply wants to sink in and let go. New to Yin or experienced, you're welcome here.

## What you need

A bolster (or a firm cushion / rolled blanket) and one or two yoga blocks (books work in a pinch). A blanket nearby is lovely too, plus a warm, quiet space where you can be still. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to a real stretch — a gentle challenge — but never pain, and never force. Let the props carry you.
- **Hold for time.** These are long, supported holds; the length is where the release happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath.
