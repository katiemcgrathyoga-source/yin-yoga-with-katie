## What this Yin class is

A full hour of Yin for the lower body — a deep, grounding stretch through the hips, legs and spine. We move slowly through Child's Pose, Dragon, Sleeping Swan and a Shoelace fold and twist on each side, then open the spine with Melting Heart, Supported Fish and a Reclined Twist before resting. It's a lovely way to release tension from tight hips and a stiff back, gain a little flexibility, and simply unwind. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone carrying tension in the hips, legs or lower back — after long days at a desk, on your feet, or when everything below the waist feels tight. It's also a calm, meditative hour if you just want to slow down and relax. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. A yoga block or two is handy — under the hips in Child's Pose, or under the back in Supported Fish — though a cushion works just as well. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held at least a couple of minutes; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on the sensations and your breath.
