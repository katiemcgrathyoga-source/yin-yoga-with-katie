## What this Yin class is

A gentle 25 minutes of Yin to close the day and carry you toward sleep. We begin sitting quietly with a few minutes of box breathing to calm the mind and shift into nighttime mode, then move through slow, soothing shapes — Sleeping Swan, a shoelace shoulder and neck release, a soft side-body stretch and some easy windshield wipers — each held long enough to let the tension of the day drain away. No props, no rush. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, and you choose what feels right tonight.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to switch off and settle before bed — whether Yin is brand new to you or already part of your evenings. If your mind is busy, your shoulders are up around your ears, or you're feeling wound up and restless, this class meets you where you are. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and somewhere warm, dim and quiet where you can be still. No props required — though a cushion or block tucked under the hip in Swan is a lovely bit of extra support if you'd like it. Dim the lights, do this right before bed, and press pause any time you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. Softer is perfect before bed.
- **Hold for time.** Stay a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, follow your breath as it slows, and come out slowly when it's time.
