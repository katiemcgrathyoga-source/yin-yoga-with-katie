## What this Yin class is

A gentle half-hour of Yin to end the day lying down. We stay fully reclined the whole way through — a bolster and a block or two supporting you underneath — so you can release the tension you've gathered without ever having to hold yourself up. Slow, supported stretches, each held for a few minutes, to soften the body and settle you toward sleep. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, and you choose what feels right tonight.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to switch off at the end of the day, especially if you love a supported, cushioned practice. Whether you're new to Yin or already have a steady practice, staying reclined makes this an easy, forgiving way to wind down. If you feel wound up, tired, or restless before bed, this class meets you where you are.

## What you need

A yoga block or two, and a bolster if you have one — a firm cushion or a couple of folded blankets work just as well. Have them close by so you can slide them into place as we go. Then find somewhere warm and quiet where you can be still, dim the lights, and press pause any time you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. Softer is perfect before bed.
- **Hold for time.** Stay a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Feel the support of the props and come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Let the body and mind settle, and follow your breath as it slows.
