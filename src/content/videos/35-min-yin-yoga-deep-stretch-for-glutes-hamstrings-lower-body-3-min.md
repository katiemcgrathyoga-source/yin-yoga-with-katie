## What this Yin class is

A deep 35-minute Yin practice for the whole lower body — the glutes, hamstrings and hips. We work through long, slow folds and one gentle reclined twist, holding each pose for about three minutes so the deeper tissue has time to soften and let go. The first half is seated; the second half is on your back. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, so you can meet your body wherever it is today.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to release tight hips, glutes and hamstrings — whether you sit at a desk all day, run or cycle, or simply feel a bit stuck through the lower body. It's an all-levels class, so newer students are very welcome; read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace and take the gentler option whenever you need it.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. No props required — but because the first half is seated, a block, cushion or folded blanket to sit on makes the folds more comfortable, and a rolled blanket under a sensitive knee is a nice touch. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** We stay around three minutes in each pose; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — if your thoughts wander, gently come back.
