## What this Yin class is

Day 23 of the Bedtime Yin Challenge is all about going inward and retreating — a soft, soothing practice to help you let go of the day. You'll settle into a long-held sleeping swan on each side, resting your body onto a bolster, ease open the heart and shoulders in puppy pose, then melt into slow reclined twists before drawing both knees in and finally spreading out to rest. It's quiet, unhurried, and made to leave you calm and ready for sleep.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to unwind and stretch gently at the end of the day, whether you're brand new to Yin or well practised. It's especially lovely if your mind tends to race at bedtime — the long holds and calm cues give you somewhere soft to land. Follow along at your own pace and take the gentler option whenever you need it.

## What you need

A bolster is nice for this one, and a yoga block to prop it up, but a couple of firm pillows or cushions work beautifully too — and if you have nothing to hand, you can simply do the poses as they are. A folded blanket under the knees is welcome in puppy pose. Find a warm, quiet space, and know that every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, so you can make each shape your own.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
