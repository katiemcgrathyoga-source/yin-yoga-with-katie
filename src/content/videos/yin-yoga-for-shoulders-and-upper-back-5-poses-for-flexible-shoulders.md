## What this Yin class is

A 36-minute Yin practice to help open tight, stiff shoulders and free up your upper back. We move the shoulder joint in every direction with five simple poses — starting with Melting Heart to open the front of the shoulders and chest, rolling through Bow Tie and Broken Wing for the sides, and finishing with Eagle and Cow-Face arms to reach all the way around. Each pose is held long and slow, so the shape can quietly do its work. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, and you decide which feels right today.

## Who this class is for

Anyone carrying tension across the shoulders and upper back — after long days at a desk, hunched over a phone, or any time the area feels tight and stiff. It's lovely if you want to build a little more flexibility and ease through the shoulders without rushing. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace and rest whenever you need to.

## What you need

Your mat, and a strap if you have one — we use it for the cow-face arms near the end, though you can reach your hands towards each other instead. A yoga block or cushion is nice for resting your forehead in the opening poses, and folding the front of your mat gives the knees a little extra padding. None of it is essential, so work with whatever you have nearby.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch through the shoulders, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; let gravity do the work. Come out slowly, and sooner if you feel any tingling or it becomes too intense.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, rest your attention on your breath, and let each shape gently soften the area over time.
