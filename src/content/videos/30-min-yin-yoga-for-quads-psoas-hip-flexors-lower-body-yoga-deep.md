## What this Yin class is

A 30-minute lower-body Yin practice focused on the quads, psoas and hip flexors — the muscles that hold so much tension from sitting and walking. We begin in dragon to open the front of the hips on both sides, move into half saddle to stretch each quad in turn, soften into a reclined butterfly for the groin and inner thighs, then come into a full saddle to open both quads together before resting in savasana. It's slow, deep and quietly releasing.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to free up tight quads and hip flexors — runners, cyclists, and anyone who sits a lot will feel this in the best way. It's suitable for all levels, with gentler and deeper options offered throughout, so you can meet the practice wherever your body is today.

## What you need

A couple of yoga blocks and a cushion or pillow are handy — blocks under the hands in dragon, a cushion to sit on in saddle — but nothing here is essential, and folding your mat gives your knees extra padding if you need it. Find a warm, quiet space where you can be still. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, so take whichever feels right.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
