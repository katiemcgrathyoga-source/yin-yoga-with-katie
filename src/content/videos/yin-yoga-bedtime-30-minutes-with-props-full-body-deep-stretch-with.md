## What this Yin class is

A gentle half-hour of Yin to close the day, with your props doing the holding for you. We rest over a bolster and blocks through four long, deep shapes — a supported child's pose, a melting heart, a folded caterpillar, and a supported fish — held for a few minutes each so your body can soften and let go. The props mean you can be fully passive: settle your weight, let the shape do the work, and drift toward sleep. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, and you choose what feels right tonight.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to wind down before bed with a little extra support under them — whether you're new to Yin or already have a practice. If you feel tired, wound up, or achy at the end of the day, resting over a bolster makes the stretches feel comforting rather than effortful. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace.

## What you need

Your mat and a bolster, or a couple of yoga blocks — or both, if you have them. Don't worry if you only have one or the other; a firm cushion works just as well. Have a blanket nearby for warmth, dim the lights, and settle somewhere quiet where you can be still. Adjust your props and yourself as much as you need — there's no rush to get into any pose. Press pause any time.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. With props you can hold back even more; softer is perfect before bed.
- **Hold for time.** Stay a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Let body and mind settle over your props, and follow your breath as it slows.
