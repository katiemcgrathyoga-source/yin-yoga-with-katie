## What this Yin class is

A slow, 40-minute Yin practice for those days when your back feels tired, tight, or a little sore. We work gently through the back, spine and hips — starting seated in a folded Butterfly, opening the upper back and shoulders with Eagle arms, then finding a deep spinal stretch in Snail or Caterpillar, a soft backbend in Seal, and long, quiet twists to finish. Each pose is held for a few minutes so the shape can do the work while you soften and breathe. It's an unhurried release that may help ease the everyday tension a busy back tends to carry.

## Who this class is for

Anyone whose back feels tight or worn out and would love to lie down, slow right down, and let things settle. It's gentle enough for beginners — every pose has a lighter option and a deeper one, so you choose how far to go — and there are plenty of quiet moments to calm the mind, because a sore back can feel just as stressful as it does physical. A note of care: if your pain is sharp, ongoing, or travels down a leg, please see a health professional first. This is a soft, supportive practice, not a fix for a back condition or injury.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. No props are required, though a cushion or folded blanket nearby is welcome — to sit up on in the seated poses, or to rest under your knees in the twists. Come out of any shape slowly, and sooner if you need to. Let comfort lead the way.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. With time that edge may soften and let you fold a little deeper.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a few minutes each; let the shape do the work. Come out gently, and sooner if the sensation asks you to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind and rest your attention on your breath — letting each slow exhale invite a little more ease into your back.
