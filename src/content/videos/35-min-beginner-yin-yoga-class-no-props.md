## What this Yin class is

A calm, beginner-friendly 35 minutes of Yin with no props at all. We settle into the hips with butterfly and a wide-legged straddle, rest in child's pose, open the spine with sphinx, and finish with a long reclined twist before resting in corpse pose. Along the way I'll share the three simple principles of Yin — finding your edge, stillness and time — so you leave feeling stretched, softened and a little more at ease.

## Who this class is for

This is the perfect place to start if you're new to Yin or new to yoga altogether — you are in a safe place here. It's also lovely for anyone who wants a slow, gentle stretch to unwind and quiet the mind, with a calm voice guiding you the whole way through.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. This is a no-props class, though a cushion nearby is nice to sit on or rest under if you'd like a little more support. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, so make it your own — and pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
