## What this Yin class is

A short, soothing class devoted entirely to your feet and ankles. We settle into a held toe squat to open the soles of the feet, ease into an ankle stretch, then wake everything up with a gentle down dog, some toe stretches and ankle rotations, and finish with a proper foot massage — a little way to show your feet some TLC. It's a gentle, grounding practice that leaves tired feet feeling rejuvenated and light.

## Who this class is for

Anyone whose feet are feeling tired, tight or overworked — if you walk, run, stand all day or simply never give your feet any attention, this is for you. It's beginner-friendly and calm from start to finish, and every shape has a softer option if you need it.

## What you need

Just your mat and a little space to sit. Toe squat can be strong, so a cushion or yoga block to sit on and a folded blanket under your knees are both nice to have — all optional. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, so take whichever feels right for your feet today.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
