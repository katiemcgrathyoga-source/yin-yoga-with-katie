## What this Yin class is

A short and playful insect-inspired Yin sequence to open the hips, lengthen the spine and ease out the hamstrings. We begin in dragonfly — folding gently to one side, then the other, then straight down the middle — before moving into a long caterpillar fold and finishing softly on the back in reclined butterfly. Just twenty minutes of quiet, slow stretching to leave you feeling loose and settled.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants a manageable, feel-good stretch for the spine, hips and hamstrings without needing a full hour. It suits beginners and seasoned practitioners alike, and it's a lovely one to slot into a busy day when you still want to move slowly and mindfully.

## What you need

Just your mat, and ideally something to sit up on — a cushion or yoga block — so your pelvis can tip forward comfortably in dragonfly and caterpillar. A rolled blanket or a couple of cushions to support the knees is nice if your hamstrings feel tight. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, so take whichever your body is asking for.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
