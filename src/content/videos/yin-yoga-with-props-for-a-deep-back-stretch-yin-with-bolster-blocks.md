## What this Yin class is

A calm, fully-supported Yin class for the back and spine, using a bolster and one or two blocks so you can settle in and let go. We begin folding forward over the bolster in caterpillar, lift into a supported bridge on blocks, then release the lower back by hugging the knees in over the bolster. From there we open the chest in a supported fish, wring out the whole spine in a reclined twist on both sides, and rest in a propped corpse pose. Each pose is held for three to four minutes, so tension has time to melt away.

## Who this class is for

Anyone whose back and spine feel tight, stiff or tired and wants a slow, gentle way to unwind them. Because everything is supported by props, it's welcoming whether you're new to Yin or well practised. It's especially lovely at the end of a long day or a desk-bound week.

## What you need

A bolster and one or two yoga blocks, plus a warm, quiet space where you can be still. A firm cushion or a couple of pillows can stand in for the bolster, and a spare pillow is handy for resting your head in fish pose. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one — take whichever your body asks for, and press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
