## What this Yin class is

Day 19 of the Bedtime Yin Challenge — a gentle 30-minute full-body stretch to help you unwind and let go of the day before sleep. We settle in with the breath, hold a long half-butterfly for the hamstrings and lower back, then ease the upper back and shoulders with thread-the-needle and a restful child's pose, before finishing in a slow reclined twist to release the spine. Slow, still and calming — the perfect way to end your day.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who'd like to relax, stretch and quiet the mind before bed. It's a lovely evening wind-down whether you're brand new to Yin or well practised, and it fits beautifully into the 30-day bedtime challenge. New to this? Simply follow the shapes at your own pace and take the gentler option whenever you need it.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still — ideally somewhere you can drift off afterwards. This is a no-props class, though a cushion or folded blanket nearby is nice to sit on or rest under if you'd like a little more support. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, so make it yours.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
