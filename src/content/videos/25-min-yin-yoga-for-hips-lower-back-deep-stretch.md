## What this Yin class is

A 25-minute Yin practice to open the hips and release the lower back — a shorter sequence you can come back to whenever your body feels tight. We begin seated in folded butterfly, move through half-butterfly on each side, then settle into sleeping swan for the outer hips and glutes. From there we turn over into sphinx for a soft stretch through the lower back, rest in crocodile, and finish in a long, quiet child's pose. Each shape is held around two minutes so you can soften in slowly and let the deeper tension unwind. Come as you are — every pose has a lighter option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone carrying tightness in the hips or lower back — especially if you sit a lot during the day and want to feel loose and easy again. It's short enough to fit into a busy evening but deep enough to really let things go, so it's lovely before bed or any time your back and hips are asking for some space. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace and go only as far as feels good.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props are required — though a cushion or block is always welcome if you'd like to sit up a little higher, support a knee, or rest your head. Press pause whenever you need to, and let yourself stay longer in anything that feels nice.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. Let your body invite you deeper over time.
- **Hold for time.** Each pose is held a while; let the shape do the work rather than pushing. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Soften your shoulders and jaw, and rest your attention on your breath — a gentle anchor to keep you here on your mat.
