## What this Yin class is

A full hour of Yin to stretch the whole body and truly relax — Class 3 of the Create Peaceful Moments series, though it stands perfectly well on its own. We begin seated with alternate nostril breathing to settle the mind, then move slowly through the shoulders with a supported release and Broken Wing, open the hips with Deer, Swan and Dragon, and wind down on our backs before a long, restful Savasana. The holds are generous, so there's plenty of time to soften and let go. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants an unhurried, full-body stretch that leaves you calmer than it found you — lovely at the end of a busy day or any time you need to drop out of your head. It's a friendly hour for newer students too; nothing is rushed, and I talk you into and out of every shape. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace.

## What you need

Your mat and two yoga blocks nearby — we use them to support the shoulders and lift the hips, though a cushion works just as well. A warm, quiet space where you can be still, and a blanket close by if you tend to cool down in the final rest. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or so; let the shape do the work, and come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath and the sensations as they arise.
