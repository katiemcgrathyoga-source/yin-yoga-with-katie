## What this Yin class is

A full hour of Yin to create some peaceful moments. We open with a gentle 4:8 breathing exercise to settle the nervous system, then move slowly through a full-body sequence — folding forward, opening the shoulders and upper back, softening into the hips, and unwinding through long reclined twists. I name each pose and how long we'll stay, then let you sink into the hold. This is class six of the Create Peaceful Moments series, but it stands completely on its own. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to give themselves a proper hour on the mat — to stretch the whole body, quieten the mind, and drop out of a busy week. It's lovely after a long day, at the weekend, or any time you're craving a slower, more meditative practice. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. This is a no-props class, so nothing is required — though it's always nice to have a couple of blocks or a cushion on standby, in case you'd like extra padding under your knees or something to rest your head on. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on the sensations and your breath.
