## What this Yin class is

Ninety minutes of silent Yin to nourish the belly. The poses gently compress and twist the abdomen — massaging the digestive organs and stimulating the Stomach, Spleen and Kidney lines — while long holds let everything settle. Because it's a silent class, I keep the cues to a minimum so you can drop fully into each shape and out of your head. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to support their digestion and gut health, or who simply loves a long, quiet, meditative practice with very little talking. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. A cushion or blanket nearby is lovely for support. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on the sensations and your breath — especially helpful in a silent practice.
