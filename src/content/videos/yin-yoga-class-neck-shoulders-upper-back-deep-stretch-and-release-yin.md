## What this Yin class is

A 30-minute Yin practice for the neck, shoulders and upper back — the places so many of us hold the day without noticing. We begin seated, with a few quiet moments to settle and some slow neck stretches, then move into Puppy and a little Cat-Cow to open across the upper back. From there we soften into Broken Wing, rest in Crocodile, and unwind the shoulders in Bow Tie (sometimes called pretzel arms), before closing the way we started — still and easy. Each shape is held long and slow, so tension has time to release rather than being stretched away in a hurry. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone whose neck and shoulders feel tight, tense or worn from sitting, screens or a long day. It's a short, unhurried practice you can come to in the evening to unwind, or any time the upper body feels wound up and asking for space. New to Yin? You're very welcome here — read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace and let yourself settle.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for half an hour. No props are required — though if sitting on the floor feels hard on the hips, a cushion or block under you is welcome, and in Puppy or Bow Tie a little padding under the chest or forehead can help you relax more fully. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch through the neck, shoulders and upper back, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; let the shape and your breath do the work. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Soften the jaw and face, let the shoulders drop away from the ears, and rest your attention on your breath.
