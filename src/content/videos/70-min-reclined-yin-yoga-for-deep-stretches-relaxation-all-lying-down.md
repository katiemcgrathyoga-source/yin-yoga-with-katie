## What this Yin class is

Seventy minutes of Yin with your body fully supported by the floor — every pose is lying down, so there's nothing to hold up and nowhere to be. Long, slow stretches unwind the hips, spine and legs while the whole body settles toward rest. It's a beautiful one for a tired body or the end of the day. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to stretch deeply *and* deeply relax — especially if you're tired, tense, or short on energy. New to Yin or well into your practice, you're welcome. Because you stay on the floor throughout, it's gentle and accessible.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. A cushion or blanket nearby is lovely for a little extra support. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to a real stretch — a gentle challenge — but never pain, and never force. Your edge is different in every body, and every day.
- **Hold for time.** These are long, supported holds, and that's where the release deepens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and let your attention rest on your breath.
