## What this Yin class is

A short, gentle introduction to Yin Yoga — your very first class, if you like. We move slowly through just a handful of simple full-body poses, holding each one for two minutes so the stretch can reach past the muscle into the deeper tissue around your joints. I talk you into and out of every shape step by step, so there's nothing to figure out on your own. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Complete beginners, and anyone easing back into their body after a break. If you've never done Yin — or any yoga — before, this is a lovely place to start. There's no experience needed, nothing to keep up with, and no way to get it wrong. Just follow my voice and go at your own pace.

## What you need

Your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a few minutes. A cushion or a couple of blocks to sit on makes the seated poses more comfortable, and a blanket is nice to have nearby — but none of it is essential. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. If it's sharp or painful, back off.
- **Hold for time.** We stay two minutes in each pose and let the shape do the work. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, soften your breath, and let yourself rest into each shape. Come out slowly when it's time to move on.
