## What this Yin class is

Ninety minutes of deep-stretch Yin to release held tension right through your fascia. We hold each pose for a full five minutes, giving the connective tissue time to soften so you feel openness in the hips, length through the spine, and space across the shoulders and quads. I've chosen shapes that always help me find a really deep stretch — and once we're settled in each one, I keep the cues to a minimum so you can make the practice your own. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants a proper, unhurried deep stretch — to unwind tight hips and a stiff spine, or simply to sink into a long, meditative practice. It's gentle and beginner-friendly, with alternatives offered throughout, so newer students are just as welcome as seasoned ones. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace.

## What you need

Your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. Have a strap within reach for the Cow-Face arms, and keep a couple of blocks or a bolster nearby for support — the bolster is especially lovely under Saddle, and a cushion is welcome for seated poses. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** We stay around five minutes in each pose; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on the sensations and your breath.
