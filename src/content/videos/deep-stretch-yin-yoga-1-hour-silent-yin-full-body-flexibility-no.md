## What this Yin class is

A full hour of silent Yin for deep full-body flexibility — a slow, spacious stretch with minimal cues and no props needed. We open gently through the back with Child's Pose and Cat/Cow, melt the heart and shoulders open, then work along the spine through Sphinx and Seal before settling into the hips and legs with Shoelace and Happy Baby. A reclined twist and a long side-body stretch wring everything out before we rest. I name each shape and how long we'll stay, then let you sink into the silence. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants a deep, unhurried full-body stretch with very little talking — after a long day, at the end of a busy week, or any time your body feels tight and your mind feels loud. It suits all levels, from complete beginners to seasoned practitioners. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props required — though a cushion or blanket nearby is always welcome. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; the length is where the change happens. Let the shape do the work, and come out slowly.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — especially helpful in a silent practice.
