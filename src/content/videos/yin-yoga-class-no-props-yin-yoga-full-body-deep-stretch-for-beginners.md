## What this Yin class is

A gentle, beginner-friendly hour of Yin with no props at all — a full-body deep stretch to open the body and quiet the mind. We begin resting in child's pose, then fold through half butterfly, ease into a soft sphinx backbend with crocodile to rest, and finish on the back with reclined pigeon and a supine twist before settling into savasana. Each pose is held for three or four minutes, long and slow, so the stretch has time to reach those deeper connective tissues.

## Who this class is for

This is a lovely first taste of Yin if you're new to the practice, and just as welcome if you simply want a slow, no-fuss stretch for the whole body. There's no rush and nothing to reach for — just follow along at your own pace. Every pose comes with an easier option, so you can make each shape work for your body.

## What you need

Only your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. This is a no-props class, so rather than reaching for a bolster or block we adjust each pose to suit you. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one — take whichever feels right, and press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
