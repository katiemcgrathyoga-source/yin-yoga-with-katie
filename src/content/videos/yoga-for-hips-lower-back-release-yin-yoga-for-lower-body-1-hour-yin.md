## What this Yin class is

A full hour of Yin for the lower body — long, slow holds (around three to five minutes each) to release tight hips and let the lower back soften. If the day collects in your hips and back, this is where it lets go. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, and you choose what feels right today.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who sits a lot, feels tight through the hips, or carries tension in the lower back — whether you're brand new to Yin or well into your practice. This class meets you where you are.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. Keep a cushion or blanket nearby if you'd like to support the hips in the longer holds. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to a real stretch — a gentle challenge — but never pain, and never force. Your edge changes from body to body, and from day to day.
- **Hold for time.** These are long holds; the length is where the release happens. Always come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on the sensations and your breath.
