## What this Yin class is

Seventy minutes of Yin to open through the front of the body. We gently backbend to release tension and blockages from the chest, then work slowly into the shoulders and upper back — supported fish, melting heart, broken wing and cow-face — with long, quiet holds in between. It's a lovely counter to hours spent hunched over a desk or a phone, and heart-opening can feel surprisingly emotional too, so give yourself plenty of space. This is class three of my nine-week Yin Yoga Challenge. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who sits or rounds forward a lot through the day and wants to open the chest, shoulders and upper back. It suits all levels — whether you're following the whole Yin Yoga Challenge week by week, or you just want to undo some tension and breathe more freely. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace.

## What you need

Your mat, and a pillow or cushion and a blanket for support. A couple of yoga blocks are handy for the supported fish — a few thick books work just as well if you don't have blocks. A bolster is lovely if you have one. Set up somewhere warm and quiet, and press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. Backbends and shoulder openers can feel intense, so ease in gently.
- **Hold for time.** Stay a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Come out sooner if you need to, and come out slowly.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — letting the chest soften and open with each exhale.
