## What this Yin class is

A gentle 30-minute bedtime Yin for the back — no props, just you and your mat. We stay low and slow the whole way through, moving from a Folded Butterfly into Sphinx and Crocodile, then Child's Pose and a soft Cat-Cow, a Shoelace twist and side bend on each side, and finally knees to chest before we settle into Corpse Pose. Each shape is held a good while so gravity and your breath can do the work, helping to release a stiff or sore back as you unwind and quiet your mind. Come as you are — every pose has a lighter option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to give their back a little TLC at the end of the day. It's lovely if you sit a lot, feel tension gathering in your lower back, or you just want a calm, unhurried way to wind down before sleep. No experience needed — new to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace and let the long holds carry you.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still and comfortable. No props are needed — though a cushion or folded blanket nearby is always welcome if you'd like a little padding under your forehead, hips, or knees. Dim the lights, get cosy, and press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. In the twists especially, only go as far as that first gentle sensation.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; let the shape do the work. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Soften your jaw, your shoulders, your neck, and rest your attention on your breath — no special breathing, just noticing it come in and out.
