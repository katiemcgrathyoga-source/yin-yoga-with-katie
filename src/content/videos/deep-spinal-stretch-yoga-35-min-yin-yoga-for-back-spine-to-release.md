## What this Yin class is

A calm 35-minute journey for your back and spine, holding each shape for three to five long, quiet minutes. We fold forward in child's pose and dangling, open into gentle backbends with sphinx and seal, then hug the knees in and wring things out with a reclined twist on both sides — stretching your spine in every direction before you rest. It's a beautiful way to release aches and tension from tired back muscles and leave feeling longer and lighter.

## Who this class is for

Anyone carrying tension in their back or spine, and anyone who loves a slow, deeply restful practice. It suits all levels — if you're new to Yin, simply follow the shapes at your own pace and take the gentler option whenever you need it.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space to be still. A cushion or block nearby is nice if your hips don't reach your heels in child's pose, but nothing is required. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one — choose whichever meets your body today.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
