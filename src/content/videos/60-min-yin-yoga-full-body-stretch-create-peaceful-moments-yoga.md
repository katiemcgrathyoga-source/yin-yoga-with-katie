## What this Yin class is

A full hour of gentle Yin to create some peaceful moments — Class 7 of the Create Peaceful Moments series. We open with a few settling breaths and a round of box breathing, then move slowly through a full-body stretch using a block or two for support: a heart-opening Supported Fish, a Pontoon shape for the lower back and front of the hips, reclined twists, Dangling, a grounding squat and Thread the Needle for the shoulders. Long, supported holds let everything soften. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants a calm, restorative hour to stretch the whole body and quieten a busy mind — after a long day, at the weekend, or any time you crave a little more peace in your week. It works beautifully as a standalone class or as part of the weekly Create Peaceful Moments challenge. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Your mat, and a yoga block or two — grab those before you start so they're within reach. A cushion or blanket is lovely for extra support under the head or knees. Find a warm, quiet space where you can be still, and press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; the length is where the change happens. Let the shape do the work, and come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on the sensations and your breath.
