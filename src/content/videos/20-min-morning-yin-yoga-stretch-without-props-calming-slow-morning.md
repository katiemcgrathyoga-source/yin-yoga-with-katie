## What this Yin class is

A slow, gentle 20-minute Yin practice to ease you into the day. We settle in with a long child's pose, loosen the spine with a few rounds of cat-cow and down dog, then open the hips in dragon on both sides before drawing the breath wide in puppy pose. A soft seated twist wakes up the middle of the body, and we close with a quiet moment of stillness. No props, no rush — just a calm way to stretch away any tension that gathered overnight.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who likes to start the morning slowly and gently, whether you're brand new to Yin or well practised. It's a lovely first thing to do before the day gets going, and it works even when your body feels stiff and sleepy. Follow the shapes at your own pace and take the gentler option whenever you need it.

## What you need

Just your mat and a quiet corner where you can be still for twenty minutes. This is a no-props class, though a cushion or pillow nearby is nice to tuck under your hips in child's pose or to soften a knee. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one — choose whichever your morning body is craving.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
