## What this Yin class is

A gentle 38-minute Yin practice built entirely from cat-inspired shapes — a fun little tribute to the quiet, confident cat. We begin on the belly in Sphinx to open the chest, soften into Crocodile to rest, then move through Thread the Needle for the shoulders, Melting Heart to let the back release, a slow Cat-Cow to free the spine, and Cat Pulling Its Tail — a gentle twist through the middle and lower back — before settling into a long, quiet Savasana. Each pose is held a while and taken slow, so you can drop out of your head and simply feel. Come as you are — every shape has a lighter option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who loves an unhurried, introspective practice — and, of course, anyone who loves cats. It's a lovely one if you want to open the back and shoulders without much fuss, or if you just need a soft, playful reminder to be a little quieter and a little sillier for a while. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace and let the held times be gentle suggestions rather than rules.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props are required — though for Cat Pulling Its Tail, a cushion or bolster tucked under the shoulder can feel supportive if it doesn't quite reach the floor, so keep one nearby if you'd like. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; let the shape do the work. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on the physical sensations and your breath.
