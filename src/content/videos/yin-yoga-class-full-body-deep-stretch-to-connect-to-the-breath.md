## What this Yin class is

A gentle, unhurried full-body Yin practice filmed at the beach, where we borrow the ebb and flow of the tide as a way to get to know the breath, the body and the mind. We settle in with a long, quiet Easy Pose, then move slowly through Caterpillar, Dragon, Child's Pose, Eagle arms with a Shoelace seat, and a pair of Reclined Twists before winding down into stillness. Each shape is held for a few minutes so gravity and your breath can do the work — no rushing, no forcing, just space to notice what arises and let it wash over you.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants a deep, slow full-body stretch with a meditative, mind-quieting feel — whether you're new to Yin or come back to it often. It's lovely at the end of a full day, when the mind is busy, or any time you'd like to slow down and follow the breath for a while. New to these shapes? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along and take the gentler option wherever you need it.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. No props are required, though a cushion or yoga block under your seat is a kind touch if sitting cross-legged feels tight, and a folded blanket near the knees is always welcome. Come as you are, and press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real, honest stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; let gravity and your breath do the work, and come out slowly, sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on the breath — watching sensations and thoughts arrive and flow away, like waves.
