## What this Yin class is

A gentle evening of Yin that gives your shoulders all the attention. We melt the chest down in Melting Heart, let gravity ease the shoulders open in Bow-Tie and Broken Wing, and finish by lengthening the spine with Cow-Face Arms — each shape held for a few slow minutes so the day's tightness can quietly unwind. It's the perfect way to release the tension we carry high in the body before bed. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, and you choose what feels right tonight.

## Who this class is for

Anyone with tight shoulders or a long day behind them who wants to switch off before sleep — whether you're brand new to Yin or already have a practice. If you've been hunched at a desk, carrying stress in your neck, or you simply feel wound up at the end of the day, this class meets you where you are.

## What you need

Just your mat, and somewhere warm and quiet where you can be still. A strap or belt is lovely for the Cow-Face Arms near the end, and a yoga block is handy to rest your forehead on — but don't worry if you haven't got them, you can do the whole routine without. A cushion or blanket nearby is welcome for extra comfort. Press pause any time you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. Softer is perfect before bed.
- **Hold for time.** Stay a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Let the body and mind settle, follow your breath, and come out slowly when it's time.
