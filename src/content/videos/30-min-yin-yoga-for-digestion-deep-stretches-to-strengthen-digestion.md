## What this Yin class is

A soothing half-hour of Yin to give your digestion some TLC. The poses gently compress and twist the belly — squeezing and massaging the digestive organs to help ease bloating and cramps and get things moving again — while long, slow holds of two to three minutes let everything settle. I guide you into each shape and out again, so all you have to do is follow along. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone whose digestion needs a little support, or who simply wants a short, calming stretch that eases the belly and quiets the mind. It's lovely after a heavy meal has settled, at the end of the day, or any time you feel bloated and want to unwind. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props required — though a cushion or a couple of blocks nearby is nice for supporting the hips if you'd like it. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** We stay two to three minutes in each pose; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath as the shape gently works on the belly.
