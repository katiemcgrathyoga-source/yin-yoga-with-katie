## What this is

A 10-minute guided relaxation in Corpse Pose (Savasana) — the resting practice you feel long after it ends. There's nothing to stretch and nothing to hold. You lie down, get warm and comfortable, and let my voice guide you through a slow head-to-toe body scan, softening one part at a time until your whole body feels heavy and supported. It's the perfect way to close a Yin class, and just as lovely on its own when you simply need to switch off.

## Who this is for

Anyone who wants to rest — whether you've just finished a practice or you're winding down at the end of the day. If your mind is busy or your body feels wound up, this meets you exactly where you are. No experience needed; all you have to do is lie still and listen.

## What you need

Very little. A warm, quiet spot where you can lie down and won't be disturbed. A blanket to keep you cosy and a cushion under your head or knees are welcome if you have them, but nothing is required. Dim the lights, turn off notifications, and give yourself these ten minutes.

## How to settle in

- **Get comfortable.** Lie on your back and let your arms and legs take up as much space as they need, palms turned up to the sky.
- **Soften.** Release your jaw, your face, your shoulders — let the weight of your body sink into the floor and feel how well the ground supports you.
- **Follow the guidance.** Let my voice do the thinking. As each part of you is named, let it grow heavy and still, and simply rest until it's time to gently come back.
