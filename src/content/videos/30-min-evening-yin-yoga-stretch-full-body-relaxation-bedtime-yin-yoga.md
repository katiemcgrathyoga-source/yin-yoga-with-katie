## What this Yin class is

A calm, 30-minute evening Yin class — Day 21 of the bedtime challenge — to gently stretch the whole body and help you unwind before sleep. We settle in with a long butterfly to open the hips and groin, work into the hips and spine with a shoelace twist and some soft neck releases, then create space through the back with sphinx and seal, before resting into child's pose with side bends to finish. Slow, quiet and soothing from start to end.

## Who this class is for

Anyone looking to relax, release the day and prepare the body for sleep. It's an evening practice with a gentle, encouraging voice to guide you the whole way, and it works whether you're brand new to Yin or well practised. New to this? Simply follow the shapes at your own pace and take the gentler option whenever you need it.

## What you need

One or two yoga blocks are handy and a cushion is lovely to sit on, though nothing is essential — a folded blanket works just as well. Find a warm, quiet space where you can be still, keep the lights low, and know that every pose here has a gentler option and a deeper one. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
