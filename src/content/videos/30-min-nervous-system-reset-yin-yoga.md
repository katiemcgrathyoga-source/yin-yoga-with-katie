## What this Yin class is

A gentle 30-minute reset for a frazzled nervous system. We settle in constructive rest, ease the often-tight psoas with a supported knee-to-chest release, then open the chest in a supported fish with reclined butterfly. From there we soften the hips in reclined swan and a slow figure-4 twist, take one big full-body stretch, and melt back into constructive rest to finish. Slow, quiet and deeply calming from beginning to end.

## Who this class is for

This one is for you if you're carrying stress or anxiety, recovering from a busy week, or struggling to switch off at night. It's beginner-friendly and almost entirely reclined, so there's nothing to force and nowhere to be. Come exactly as you are.

## What you need

Two yoga blocks, and a bolster if you have one — a firm pillow or cushion works just as well for resting your head and propping your chest. A warm, quiet space where you can be still is all else you need. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, so take whichever your body wants today.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
