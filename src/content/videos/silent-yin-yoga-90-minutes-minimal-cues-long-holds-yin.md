## What this Yin class is

A full ninety minutes of silent Yin — a deep, unhurried full-body stretch held long and slow. I say just enough to guide you in: the name of each pose and how long we'll stay, then I leave you to the quiet so you can settle past the busy mind and fully into the shape. Long holds work through the hips, shoulders, back, chest and legs, one patient pose at a time. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who loves a peaceful, meditative practice with very little talking — and anyone who wants a proper, deep stretch with time and space to breathe. It's a beautiful one for a slow morning or a quiet evening. New to Yin? Have a read of the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Your mat, a warm and quiet space where you can be still, and — for the Supported Fish near the end — a bolster or firm cushion to rest back over. A blanket nearby is lovely for the long holds. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; the length is where the change happens. My times are only suggestions — come out sooner whenever you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on the sensations and your breath — especially helpful in a silent practice.
