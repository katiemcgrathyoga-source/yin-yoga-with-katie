## What this Yin class is

A gentle 30-minute journey of soft, supported backbends to open the chest and shoulders and bring some flexibility back to the spine. We start reclined over a block in supported fish, then move onto the belly for sphinx, broken wing, puppy and seal — each one gently opening the front of the body — before settling with a simple seated twist to rebalance. It's the perfect antidote if you've been spending your days folded forward and hunched over.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to counter the effects of sitting, driving or working with their head down, and would love to open up through the chest and shoulders. It suits all levels — the backbends stay gentle and every pose offers a way to soften or deepen — so come as you are, whether you're new to Yin or well practised.

## What you need

A couple of yoga blocks (or two thick books) for the supported fish and the deeper puppy pose, plus your mat and a warm, quiet space. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, so take whichever feels right for your body today and rest whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
