## What this Yin class is

A gentle 45 minutes of Yin to calm your nervous system and let stress drain away. We open with a simple breathing exercise — alternate nostril breathing — to quieten the mind, then move through a small handful of soft, supported shapes: supported bridge and supported fish over blocks, a long caterpillar fold, and legs up the wall. Several of these poses gently encourage blood towards the head, which has a wonderfully calming effect. There's nothing to force here — you settle in, let gravity and your breath do the work, and be still. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who feels wound up, frazzled, or anxious and wants to downshift — after a long day, before bed, or any time you need to drop out of your head and back into your body. It's gentle and fully supported, so it suits all levels, including complete beginners. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace.

## What you need

A couple of yoga blocks or some thick books, and a blanket or scarf to lay over them so everything stays soft. You'll also want a little clear wall space nearby for legs up the wall near the end. A cushion for sitting on is lovely too. Keep warm and cosy, make yourself as undisturbable as possible, and press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Stay a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath.
