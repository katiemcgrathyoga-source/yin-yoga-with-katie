## What this Yin class is

A gentle 30-minute bedtime practice from Day 17 of the Bedtime Yin challenge, built around soft inversions — poses where your hips rest a little higher than your head. We melt into puppy and child's pose, let everything hang in dangling, settle over your blocks in supported bridge, then release the lower back and rest. Each shape draws the mind quiet and encourages a calming, settled feeling — perfect for unwinding before sleep.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to switch off at the end of the day and drift towards a deeper night's sleep. It's calm, well supported and beginner-friendly, so it suits you whether you're brand new to Yin or well practised. Lovely as the last thing you do before climbing into bed.

## What you need

One or two yoga blocks (or anything that lifts your hips) for supported bridge, your mat, and a warm, quiet space to be still. If your knees are sensitive, fold your mat or add a blanket for padding under puppy pose. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one — take whichever feels right tonight.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
