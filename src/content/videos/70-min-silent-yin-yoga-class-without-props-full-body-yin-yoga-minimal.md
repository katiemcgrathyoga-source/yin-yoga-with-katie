## What this Yin class is

A full 70 minutes of silent Yin — a deep full-body stretch with minimal cues and no props needed. I say just enough to guide you into each shape and tell you how long we'll stay, then leave you to the quiet so you can drop out of your head and fully into the pose. Long, slow holds work through the feet and legs, the back and spine, the shoulders and hips. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who loves a peaceful, meditative practice with very little talking — and anyone who wants a proper deep stretch without gathering any props. It's a lovely way to unwind at the end of the day or settle before sleep. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props required — though a cushion or blanket nearby is always welcome to pad sensitive knees or rest your head. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. Let the shape do the work.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; the length is where the change happens. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — especially helpful in a silent practice.
