## What this Yin class is

A gentle introduction to Yin Yoga, with no props and every pose held for just one or two minutes. We begin with a short grounding meditation, then move slowly through child's pose and puppy to open the chest and spine, into a wide straddle and deer pose for the hips, and finish standing with squat and dangling before a long, restful savasana. It's a calm, full-body stretch designed to release tension and teach you the feel of Yin from the very first pose.

## Who this class is for

This one is made for beginners, or for anyone who wants a slower, simpler practice with plenty of guidance along the way. Katie explains the three principles of Yin as you move, so you'll come away understanding not just what to do but why. It's just as welcome if you're returning to your mat after a break.

## What you need

Just your mat and a quiet, warm space where you can be still. This is a no-props class, though a folded blanket for under your head or knees is nice if you'd like a little extra comfort. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one — take whichever your body is asking for today, and press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
