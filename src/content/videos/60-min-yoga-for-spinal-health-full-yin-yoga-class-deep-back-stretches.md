## What this Yin class is

A full hour of Yin devoted to the spine — slow, deep back stretches held long enough for the whole back body to soften and release. It's Class 2 of a weekly series, so you can return to it again and again. We move gently from the hamstrings up through the spine and finish with a quiet twist. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, and you choose what feels right today.

## Who this class is for

Everyone is welcome, whatever your experience. If you sit a lot, feel tight through the back, or simply want a long, calming stretch for the spine, this class meets you where you are. New to Yin? You're in exactly the right place.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. Keep a cushion or blanket nearby if you'd like a little support under the hips or knees. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. Your edge is different in every body, and every day.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while. My hold times are only suggestions; always listen to your body and come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle both body and mind, and rest your attention on the sensations and your breath.
