## What this Yin class is

Seventy minutes of Yin to stretch you from head to toe — the final class of my Yin Yoga Challenge, and a lovely one to return to any time you want a full, deep release. We open with a grounding meditation, then work slowly through the whole body: the feet and ankles, the back and hips, a long stretch through the legs, the shoulders, and finally some gentle twists to unwind the spine before we rest. The holds are long, so there's plenty of time to settle and let each shape do the work. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants a proper, unhurried full-body stretch — whether you've followed the whole challenge or you're dropping in fresh. It's a wonderful weekly reset, and a calming way to release tension through the hips, legs and spine. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. A cushion, a folded blanket and a block are lovely to have nearby — we'll use them to sit up on and to pad the knees and ankles wherever you need more comfort. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, rest your attention on your breath, and come out slowly when it's time.
