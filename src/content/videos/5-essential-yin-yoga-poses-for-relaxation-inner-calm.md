## What this Yin class is

This is a calm, unhurried Yin Yoga practice built around five of the most relaxing poses, held for a few minutes each. You'll begin lying down in Sphinx and Crocodile to soothe the back and settle the breath, melt into Child's Pose, then open the hips through Swan, Sleeping Swan and Shoelace with a gentle twist and side bend on both sides. It all closes with a long, restful Corpse Pose so you can soak up a real sense of inner calm.

## Who this class is for

This is a beginner-friendly class and a beautiful choice if you're feeling stressed, wired or simply in need of a reset. You don't need any experience or flexibility to enjoy it — just a willingness to slow down. It's a wonderful practice for the end of a busy day.

## What you need

No props are needed — only your mat and a little quiet space. If you'd like extra softness, a cushion or blanket under your knees, hips or forehead is always welcome. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper option, so you can meet your body exactly where it is today.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
