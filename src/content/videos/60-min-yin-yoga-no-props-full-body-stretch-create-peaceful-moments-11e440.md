## What this Yin class is

An hour to yourself. This is class nine of Create Peaceful Moments — a series about taking sixty quiet minutes to stretch, release tension and simply be still. We begin with a breathing exercise, wake the spine up with a few gentle stretches and cat-cow, then move into long, settled Yin holds: Swan and half-shoelace for the hips and hamstrings, butterfly, eagle arms for the shoulders, and a reclined twist to finish. No props, nowhere to be. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants a full, unhurried hour of Yin to unwind the whole body and quiet the mind. It's lovely after a busy week, or any time you want to carve out a peaceful moment for yourself. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. No props are required, though a block, cushion or bolster nearby can be nice for a little extra support. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a few minutes; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on the sensations and your breath.
