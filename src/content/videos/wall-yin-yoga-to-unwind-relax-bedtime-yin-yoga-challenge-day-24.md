## What this Yin class is

A calm, restful evening of wall Yin — Day 24 of the Bedtime Challenge — where the wall carries the weight and you simply let go. After a gentle shoulder opener we bring the legs up the wall and stay a while, then work slowly through the hips and groin with dragonfly, happy baby, a figure-four swan on each side, and a wide butterfly. It's soft, quiet and deeply soothing — the perfect way to unwind before sleep.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to relax, stretch out the hips and legs, and settle down for the night — especially at the end of a long day. Because the wall supports you throughout, it's welcoming for complete beginners and just as lovely for seasoned practitioners. If you're new, follow along at your own pace and take the gentler option any time you need it.

## What you need

A bit of clear wall space to get your legs up, and a bolster, cushion or some folded blankets to tuck under your hips for support. That's it — plus a warm, quiet spot where you can be still. Every pose here has a gentler option and a deeper one, so make each shape feel right for your body and press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
