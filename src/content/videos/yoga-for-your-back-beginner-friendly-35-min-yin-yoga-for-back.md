## What this Yin class is

A gentle 35-minute Yin practice for your back — a beginner-friendly way to loosen up, release some tension, and, with time, cultivate a little more ease and mobility through the spine. We move slowly through a small handful of shapes: a soft forward fold in child's pose, a supported back bend through sphinx and seal, a lateral opening in banana pose, and a quiet reclined twist to finish. Each pose is held for around three minutes, so there's plenty of time to settle, adjust, and let the shape do the work. Come as you are — every pose has a lighter option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone whose back feels tight or a bit stiff and who'd love an unhurried, low-pressure way to unwind it — especially if you're new to Yin. It's a calm choice after a long day at a desk, or any time you want to give your back some gentle attention. If your back is sore, keep everything soft and stay well within your comfort; and if any pain is sharp, ongoing, or travels down a leg, please see a health professional before you practise.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for half an hour. No props are required, though this practice is really lovely with a little support — a cushion, folded blanket, or block nearby can go under your forehead, chest, or hips whenever you'd like a softer landing. Press pause any time you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch you can feel, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while — around three minutes here — so let the shape do the work. Come out slowly, and sooner if your body asks.
- **Be still.** Soften your jaw and face, settle body and mind, and rest your attention on the breath — each inhale opening a little more, each exhale letting some tension go.
