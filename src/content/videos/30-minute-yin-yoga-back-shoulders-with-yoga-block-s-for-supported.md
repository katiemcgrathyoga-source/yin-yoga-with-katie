## What this Yin class is

A gentle half-hour of Yin to release tension from the back and shoulders and gain a little flexibility along the way. We warm the spine with child's pose and puppy, resting face-down in crocodile, then move through sphinx and seal to work the back before opening each shoulder with a broken wing. We finish with a supported fish over a block — a really lovely way to open the chest and get a deep stretch into the upper back — and a soft neck release to close.

## Who this class is for

Anyone carrying tension in their upper back or shoulders, whether from long days at a desk or simply from life. It suits all levels: there's a gentler and a deeper option in every pose, so you can keep it soft with sphinx or take it further into seal whenever you feel ready.

## What you need

Your mat and one yoga block — mainly for the supported fish at the end. No block? A thick book, a firm cushion or a pillow does the job beautifully, and a second prop under your head is a nice extra. Every pose here has a gentler option and a deeper one, so take whichever serves you today.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
