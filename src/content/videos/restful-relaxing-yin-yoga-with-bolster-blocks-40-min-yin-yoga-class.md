## What this Yin class is

Forty minutes of gentle, supported Yin to help you unwind at the end of the day. We use a bolster and blocks so your body is fully held in every shape — which means you can soften, stop trying, and let the props do the work. The holds are long and restful, releasing the hips, spine and chest while your nervous system quietly downshifts. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to lie on the floor for a while, stretch, and let the day go. It's perfect after a busy day, before bed, or any time you feel wound up and want to calm your mind. Beginners are very welcome — the props keep everything supported, so there's no forcing anywhere. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace.

## What you need

Your mat, a yoga bolster, and one or two blocks. No bolster? A stack of firm pillows or folded cushions works beautifully. A warm, quiet space where you can be still is lovely too. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Stay a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath.
