## What this Yin class is

A gentle half-hour of Yin to close the day and stretch the whole body. Tonight we move through just three shapes — Thread the Needle for the shoulders and upper back, Half-Butterfly for the hips and hamstrings, and Cat Pulling Its Tail for a soft spinal twist — each held for a few slow minutes and worked on both sides, before we rest in stillness. Do it in dim light, right before bed. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, and you choose what feels right tonight.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to unwind and let the day go before sleep — whether you're brand new to Yin or already have a practice. This is Day 5 of the Bedtime Challenge, but it stands perfectly well on its own. If you feel wound up, tired, or restless in the evening, this class meets you where you are.

## What you need

Just your mat, and somewhere warm and quiet where you can be still. A cushion or folded blanket is lovely to pad the knees, and a couple of blocks can support your forehead in the forward folds — but none of it is required. Press pause any time you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. Softer is perfect before bed.
- **Hold for time.** Stay a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Let the body and mind settle, and follow your breath as it slows. When we finish, come out slowly.
