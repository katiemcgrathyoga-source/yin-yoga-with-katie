## What this Yin class is

A gentle half-hour of Yin to close the day, with the focus on your hips, legs and spine. We settle into a few long, slow holds — a wide dragonfly forward fold, a knees-to-chest hug for the lower back, and a long banana stretch down each side of the body — each held for a few minutes so gravity can do the work and the day can drain out of you. Do it in dim light, right before bed. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, and you choose what feels right tonight.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to unwind before sleep and free up tight hips, heavy legs and a tired back — whether you're brand new to Yin or already have a practice. If you've been sitting or on your feet all day and you feel stiff, wound up or restless at night, this class meets you where you are.

## What you need

Just your mat, and somewhere warm and quiet where you can be still. A cushion or block to sit on is lovely if your hips are tight in the dragonfly, and a blanket nearby keeps you cosy as you settle. Press pause any time you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. Softer is perfect before bed.
- **Hold for time.** Stay a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Let the body and mind settle, follow your breath as it slows, and come out slowly at the end.
