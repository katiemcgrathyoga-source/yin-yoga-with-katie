## What this Yin class is

A full hour of Yin to create a little peace in your week — class four in the Create Peaceful Moments series. We begin with a simple breathing exercise, then move slowly through a full-body stretch: hips, hamstrings, spine, neck and side body, each shape held long enough to let it do its work. No props, nowhere to be — just you, your mat and an hour to yourself. Come as you are; every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to give themselves one honest hour of stretching and stillness — whether you're building a weekly Yin habit, unwinding after a busy stretch of days, or simply craving some quiet. It's an all-levels class, so newer to Yin is completely fine; just move at your own pace and take the gentler option whenever you need it.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. This is a no-props class, so there's nothing to gather — though a cushion or blanket nearby is always welcome if you'd like a little extra support. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while — around two minutes or so — and the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on the sensations and your breath.
