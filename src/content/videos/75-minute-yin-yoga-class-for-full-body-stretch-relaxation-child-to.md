## What this Yin class is

Seventy-five minutes of Yin themed around the seasons of life. We begin in Child's Pose — the embryo, the very start — and travel through the stages of a life, from childhood and the teenage years into midlife and the elder years, before settling into Corpse Pose at the end. Along the way you'll get a slow, full-body stretch, opening the spine, hips, hamstrings and chest, while long holds let your nervous system downshift. It's as much a chance to reflect and let go as it is a stretch. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants a longer, meditative practice that stretches the whole body and quiets the mind — a lovely way to reflect on where you've been and make a little space for what's next. It's a gentle, unwinding class, so it's beautiful in the evening or any time you want to slow down. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. Have a yoga block or two nearby — we use them to support the poses in the later part of the class. No blocks? Firm cushions or folded blankets do the job. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while — a couple of minutes or more — and the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on the sensations and your breath.
