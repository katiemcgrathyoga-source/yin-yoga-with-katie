## What this Yin class is

A 45-minute Yin practice for hip flexibility, working the whole hip a little at a time. We move through five long-held poses — Dragon for the hip flexors and quads, Sleeping Swan for external rotation, Butterfly for the adductors, Squat for the abductors and groin, and Deer to invite gentle internal and external rotation together. Each pose is held for a few quiet minutes, so gravity and your breath can do most of the work while you soften and stay. Come as you are — every shape has a lighter version and a deeper one, and wherever you land is perfect.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants more ease and openness through the hips — whether they feel tight from sitting, from other movement, or just from a long day. It suits all levels: newer to Yin, follow the lighter options and read the pose notes below first; more practised, take the invitation to go a little deeper when your body offers it. If a pose ever feels like too much, there's always a gentler way in, and coming back out is just as encouraged.

## What you need

Your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. A yoga block or two is lovely to have — resting your hands, forehead or hips on a block helps you settle — and a cushion or pillow does the same job if you don't have blocks. None of it is essential, though. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% — a real stretch you can feel, never forcing or pushing too deep. As long as you feel the sensation, you're doing the pose correctly.
- **Hold for time.** These poses are held a few minutes each; let gravity and your breath do the work rather than muscling in. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath or on the sensations in your hips. The goal isn't to empty your mind — just to notice when you drift and gently come back.
