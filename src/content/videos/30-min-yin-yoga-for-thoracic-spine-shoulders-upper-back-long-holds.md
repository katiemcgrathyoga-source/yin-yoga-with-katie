## What this Yin class is

Half an hour of Yin for the upper body — the thoracic spine, shoulders and upper back, an area so many of us hold tension in. We move through a handful of gentle stretches and twists, held long, that coax mobility back into the mid-spine and open the shoulders and chest. We start in Child's Pose, melt into Puppy, twist through Thread-the-Needle and Broken Wing, then finish with Supported Bridge draped over your props to let the whole spine release. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone whose shoulders and upper back feel tight and knotted — from desk days, screens, driving or stress — and anyone who wants more freedom and mobility through the mid-spine. It's all-levels: I offer a lighter version of every shape, so if Puppy Pose isn't for you today you simply stay in Child's Pose. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace.

## What you need

Your mat, and a couple of yoga blocks or books, or some firm cushions — we use them to prop the hips in Supported Bridge at the end. A blanket or folded towel is lovely under sensitive knees. Work in a warm, quiet space, and press pause whenever you need to. If you feel any tingling or numbness in your hands, ease off and come out slowly.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Stay a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath.
