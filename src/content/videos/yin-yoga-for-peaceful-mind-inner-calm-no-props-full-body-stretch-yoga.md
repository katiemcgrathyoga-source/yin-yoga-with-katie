## What this Yin class is

A gentle 41-minute Yin practice — filmed outdoors in a beautiful Taipei park — all about slowing down, tuning in and connecting to your breath. We begin with calming 4:8 breathing, ease out the neck and side body, then settle into long, quiet holds: butterfly for the hips, sphinx and seal for the spine, child's pose, a toe squat and ankle stretch, a deep squat and dangling forward fold, before a slow reclined swan into a twist and a final rest in corpse pose. The breath acts as a bridge between body and mind, leaving you calmer in the mind and more relaxed in the body.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to unwind, quiet a busy mind and give the whole body a slow, full stretch. It suits all levels — whether you're brand new to Yin or well practised — and it's especially lovely at the end of a long day. New to this? Follow the shapes at your own pace and take the gentler option whenever you need it.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for around forty minutes. This is a no-props class, though a cushion nearby is nice to sit on or rest your forehead against if you'd like a little support. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one — choose what feels right for your body today, and come out of any hold sooner if you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
