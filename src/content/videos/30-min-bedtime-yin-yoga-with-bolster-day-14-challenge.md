## What this Yin class is

A gentle half-hour of bolster Yin to close the day — slow, supported stretches where you drape your body over a bolster and let it hold your weight, so the muscles can soften and let go. Each shape is held for a few minutes to release the tension you've gathered and settle your body toward sleep. Do it in dim light, right before bed. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, and you choose what feels right tonight.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to switch off at the end of the day — whether you're new to Yin or already have a practice. The bolster does a lot of the work here, so it's especially lovely if you're tired, tender, or simply want to be held while you stretch. If you feel wound up or restless before bed, this class meets you where you are.

## What you need

A bolster is the heart of this class — but a firm cushion, a thick pillow or a couch cushion works just as well, so please don't be put off if you don't own one. A pair of yoga blocks nearby is handy if you'd like to lift the bolster a little higher, though they're entirely optional. Then just your mat, and somewhere warm and quiet where you can be still. Press pause any time you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining. Softer is perfect before bed.
- **Hold for time.** Stay a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Let the body and mind settle, and follow your breath as it slows.
