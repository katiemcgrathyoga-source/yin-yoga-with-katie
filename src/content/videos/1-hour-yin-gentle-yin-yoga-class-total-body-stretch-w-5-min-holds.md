## What this Yin class is

A slow, gentle hour of Yin for a total-body stretch — the perfect thing when you're feeling a little tired, lazy or unmotivated, but still want some mindful movement and time to unwind. We work through just a handful of grounded shapes, holding each one for around five minutes so you can settle in and let go. Nothing rushed, nowhere to be. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to chill out, loosen the hips and back, and give the whole body a long, slow stretch — after a busy day, before bed, or any time you need to drop out of your head for a while. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props are required, though a cushion or a couple of yoga blocks nearby are nice to sit on or lean into if you have them. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Stay in each shape for a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath.
