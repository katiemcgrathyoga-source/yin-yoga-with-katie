## What this Yin class is

A gentle, guided half-hour of Yin to show your upper body some love. We begin softly in child's pose and puppy pose to open the back of the shoulders, ease out the neck, then sink into two long-held shoulder openers — bow tie and broken wing — on each side. From there we unwind the spine with a reclined twist, release through the shoulders and neck, and rest completely in savasana. It's a deep stretch for the shoulders, upper back and neck, and a real chance to calm the mind.

## Who this class is for

Anyone carrying tension across their shoulders, upper back or neck — whether from a desk, a phone or a long day — and anyone who simply wants to slow down and stretch. It suits you whether you're brand new to Yin or well practised; just follow the shapes at your own pace and take the gentler option whenever you need it.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. This is a no-props class, though a cushion or block nearby is nice to slide under your hips in child's pose if sitting back on your heels feels intense. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, so make each shape your own and press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
