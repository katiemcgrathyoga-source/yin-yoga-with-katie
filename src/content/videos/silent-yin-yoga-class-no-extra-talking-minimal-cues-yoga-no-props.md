## What this Yin class is

Forty-odd minutes of silent Yin, filmed outdoors in Da'an Forest Park in Taipei — a simple full-body stretch with no extra talking. I say just enough to guide you into each shape and remind you when to come out, then leave you to the quiet. No props needed. We hold each pose for around three to five minutes, so the back, shoulders, hips and legs all get time to unwind. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants a peaceful stretch with very little talking — before bed, after a long day, or any time you need to drop out of your head. It's also a lovely one if you like the idea of a deep stretch without gathering any props. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props required — though a cushion or blanket nearby is always welcome. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Stay a few minutes and let the shape do the work. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — especially helpful in a silent practice.
