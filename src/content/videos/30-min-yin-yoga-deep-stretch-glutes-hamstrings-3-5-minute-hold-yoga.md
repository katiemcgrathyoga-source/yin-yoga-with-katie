## What this Yin class is

This is a slow, deeply targeted 30-minute Yin practice for your glutes and hamstrings, with each pose held for a full three to five minutes. We spend most of our time in shoelace — first the folded half version to find each hamstring, then the full pose to open the glutes — before releasing everything in a long, hanging dangling. A gentle downward dog links the seated work to the standing fold, and we close with a quiet moment to feel the space we've created.

## Who this class is for

It's for anyone with tight hips, glutes or hamstrings who wants a real, deep stretch delivered slowly and calmly. The pace is unhurried and every shape has options, so it suits complete beginners as well as more experienced practitioners looking to soften long-held tension in the lower body.

## What you need

Just a mat and a little floor space. Keep a cushion or a yoga block nearby to sit up on in shoelace, and a folded blanket to slide under your bottom knee if it needs padding. Every pose here has a gentler and a deeper option, so you can always find the version that's right for your body today.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
