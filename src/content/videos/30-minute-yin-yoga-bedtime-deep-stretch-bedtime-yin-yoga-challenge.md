## What this Yin class is

Day 16 of the Bedtime Yin Challenge — a full-body deep stretch to help you relax, unwind and let go before sleep. We open the hips and groin in dragon, work through the shoulders and outer hips in cow face, then melt onto our backs for a deep hamstring stretch, gentle reclined twists and cat pulling its tail, before resting in stillness. Long, slow holds to quiet a busy mind and leave the whole body feeling soft and ready for rest.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to unwind at the end of the day and sleep a little easier. It suits all levels — whether you're brand new to Yin or well practised — because every pose offers a gentler shape and a deeper one. It's a lovely way to close the day, on its own or as part of the 30-day bedtime challenge.

## What you need

A strap or belt is the main thing, with a couple of yoga blocks and a cushion nearby if you have them — all used to make the shapes more comfortable, never harder. A warm, quiet space where you can be still, and dim lighting if you can. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, so take whichever your body wants tonight and press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
