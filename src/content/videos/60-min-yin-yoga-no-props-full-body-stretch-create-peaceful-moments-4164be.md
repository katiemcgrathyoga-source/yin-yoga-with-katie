## What this Yin class is

A full hour of Yin to help you unwind — part of the Create Peaceful Moments series. We open with a settling breathing exercise, then move slowly through gentle twists, a happy baby release, a long forward fold, some spinal and neck stretches, thread-the-needle and a heart-opening melting heart, before resting in stillness at the end. No props, nowhere to be — just breath, long holds and quiet. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants a calm, full-body hour to release tension and create a little more peace in their week. It's lovely at the end of a long day, or any time you need to drop out of your head and into your body. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for an hour. No props required — though a cushion or blanket nearby is nice if you'd like to pad your knees or hips. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; let the shape do the work, and come out slowly and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath.
