## What this Yin class is

A gentle 30-minute bedtime Yin practice — the final day of the Bedtime Yin Challenge — to help you unwind, stretch and relax before sleep. We settle into the hips with a long shoelace and a soft side bend, melt into sleeping swan and rest in crocodile, then open the shoulders with broken wing and drop into child's pose — before repeating the whole sequence on the second side. No props, nowhere to be — just you, your mat and your breath as everything slows down for the night.

## Who this class is for

Anyone looking for a calm, grounding way to end the day and prepare for sleep. It's lovely whether you're brand new to Yin or well practised, and the slow pace makes it easy to follow in low light before bed. If you've been moving through the challenge with Katie, this is a beautiful one to finish on.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. This is a no-props class, though you're welcome to slide a cushion or blanket under your hips or knees if you'd like a little more support. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one — take whichever your body is asking for tonight, and come out of anything sooner if you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
