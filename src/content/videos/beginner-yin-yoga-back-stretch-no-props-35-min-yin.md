## What this Yin class is

A gentle, beginner-friendly Yin class for the whole length of your back and spine. We begin in child's pose with a soft side stretch, warm the spine with a few rounds of cat-cow, then open the upper back and chest in anahatasana before folding forward through butterfly and caterpillar to reach the lower back and hamstrings. We finish lying down in a long reclined twist, releasing any last knots of tension. It's a soothing, unhurried practice to ease stiffness and everyday aches.

## Who this class is for

Anyone new to Yin, or anyone whose back feels tight, stiff or achy and wants to move slowly and kindly. There's no experience needed and nothing to keep up with — just follow Katie's voice at your own pace. It's a lovely reset for the spine after a long day of sitting.

## What you need

Just your mat and a quiet space where you can be still for half an hour. This is a no-props class, though a cushion or pillow nearby is nice to sit on or tuck underneath you if it makes any pose more comfortable. Every pose here has a gentler option and a deeper one — choose whichever your body wants today, and pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
