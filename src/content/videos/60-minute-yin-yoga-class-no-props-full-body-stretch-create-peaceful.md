## What this Yin class is

An hour of gentle, guided Yin — class two of the Create Peaceful Moments series — to stretch the whole body and settle the mind. We open with some calming breathing, then work slowly through the hips with reclined butterfly, reclined swan and a long shoelace with eagle arms and a twist, before folding forward in caterpillar and easing out the neck and shoulders. No props, nowhere to be — just you and your mat. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants a full hour to unwind and stretch out, with a calm voice to guide you the whole way through. It's lovely after a busy day or at the end of the week, and it works whether you're brand new to Yin or well practised. New to this? Follow the shapes at your own pace and take the gentler option whenever you need it.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for an hour. This is a no-props class, though a cushion or block nearby is nice to sit on or rest under if you'd like a little more support. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
