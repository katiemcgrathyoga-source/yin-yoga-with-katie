## What this Yin class is

A 30-minute full-body Yin stretch that you can roll out anywhere, no props needed. We open the hips and groin in frog, thread the arms through for the shoulders, then move through sleeping swan, a long shoelace with eagle arms and a twist, and half-butterfly for the hamstrings — before repeating the whole sequence on the second side and easing out with a few rounds of cat–cow. It's a deep, satisfying stretch that leaves the whole body feeling loose and released.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants a proper full-body stretch in half an hour, with a calm voice guiding you the whole way. A couple of the shapes go a little deeper, so it suits you if you've practised a little Yin before — but every pose has a gentler option, so you're welcome to take it at your own pace and back off whenever you need to.

## What you need

Just your mat and a quiet space to be still. This is a no-props class, though because we begin on our knees in frog you might like to fold your mat over or tuck something soft underneath for a little padding. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one — choose whichever version feels right for your body today.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
