## What this Yin class is

A slow, low-effort 40-minute Yin practice for the days when you're tired, low on energy, or simply not in the mood for anything active — but still want a little mindful movement. These poses don't ask much of you at all. We settle in Corpse Pose, open the hips in Reclined Butterfly, fold gently through Caterpillar and Child's Pose, then unwind the spine with a reclined hamstring stretch and twist on each side. Everything is held long and slow, so you can let go of tension and just relax. Come as you are — every pose has a lighter option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to unwind without working hard for it — after a long day, when you're feeling unmotivated, or any time you'd rather lie down and breathe than push. It's gentle enough for beginners and lovely for experienced practitioners who need a restful reset. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace and come out of anything sooner if you need to.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props are required, though it's always a good idea to keep a cushion or a couple of blocks nearby — you might rest them under the knees in Reclined Butterfly or sit up on one for Caterpillar. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch you can feel, but never forcing or straining. Explore one edge at a time; there's no need to go deep straight away.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while, so let the shape do the work. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath. Each exhale is a chance to let go of a little more tension.
