## What this Yin class is

A full ninety minutes of silent Yin to give your back and spine some real TLC — long, slow holds with minimal cues and no props. I say just enough to name each shape and how long we'll stay, then leave you to the quiet so you can drop out of your head and fully into the pose. We work down through the whole back line — the legs, the hips, the length of the spine — releasing the places that hold a day's worth of tension. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone whose back is asking for some space, and anyone who loves a peaceful, meditative practice with very little talking. It's a longer class, so it suits an unhurried afternoon, a slow evening, or a rainy day when you can truly disappear into it. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace — there's no rush and nothing to keep up with.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props required — though a cushion or blanket nearby is always welcome if you'd like a little support. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — especially helpful in a silent practice.
