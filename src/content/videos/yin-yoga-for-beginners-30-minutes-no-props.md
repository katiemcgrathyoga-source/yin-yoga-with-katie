## What this Yin class is

This is a soft, unhurried 30-minute Yin practice made especially for beginners — and you won't need a single prop. You'll move gently through four long-held shapes, beginning seated in Butterfly, easing into a mellow backbend with Sphinx and a restful Crocodile, then rolling onto your back for Reclined Swan and a pair of Reclined Twists before settling into Savasana. Along the way I'll share the three simple principles of Yin, so you leave understanding not just what to do, but why it works.

## Who this class is for

This one is for you if you're brand new to Yin, or simply want something slow, calm and completely accessible. It's a lovely way to unwind, ease tight hips and a stiff back, and quiet a busy mind — whether that's a morning stretch or a way to let the day go in the evening.

## What you need

Just a mat and a little quiet space — no props at all. If you'd like extra support you can sit up on a cushion or block in Butterfly, or tuck a cushion under your hips in Reclined Swan, but none of it is essential. Every pose here has a gentler and a deeper option, so you can always find the version that feels right for your body.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
