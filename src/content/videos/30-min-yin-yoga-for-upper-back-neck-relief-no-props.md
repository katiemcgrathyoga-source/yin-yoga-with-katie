## What this Yin class is

A slow, soothing half-hour to unwind the upper back and neck — no props, nowhere to be, just you and your mat. We open the upper back with eagle arms, ease the spine with gentle seated twists and side bends, then melt down into anahatasana (melting heart) with a rest in crocodile before opening across the shoulders in bow tie. We finish long and quiet in child's pose. It's the perfect antidote to hunched shoulders and a tight, tired neck.

## Who this class is for

Anyone carrying tension in their upper back, shoulders or neck — especially after long days at a desk or on a screen. It's beginner-friendly and gently paced, so it works whether this is your first Yin class or you've been practising for years. New to Yin? Follow the shapes at your own speed and take the softer option whenever you need it.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for half an hour. This is a no-props class, though you're welcome to fold your mat over for a little padding under the knees in melting heart or child's pose. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one — choose the version that suits your body today, and press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
