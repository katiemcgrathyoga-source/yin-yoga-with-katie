## What this Yin class is

A calming 30-minute bedtime Yin class with a gentle focus on the hips — the perfect way to unwind before sleep. We begin seated with some soothing 4:8 breathing to settle the mind, then lie back for reclined butterfly, reclined swan and a reclined twist on each side, before melting into a long, restful corpse pose. Every shape is held slow and still, with no props needed, so you can let the tension of the day drain away.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to ease tight hips and quieten a busy mind before bed. It's a beginner-friendly routine with just a few poses, so it's lovely whether you're brand new to Yin or simply want something soft and grounding at the end of the day. Come to it tired — that's rather the point.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can lie down and be still. This is a no-props class, though a cushion under your head or knees is nice if you'd like a little more support. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, so take whichever feels right tonight.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
