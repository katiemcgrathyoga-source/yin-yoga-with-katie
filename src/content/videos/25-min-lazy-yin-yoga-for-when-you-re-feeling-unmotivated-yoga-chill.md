## What this Yin class is

A soft, cosy Yin class for the days when your energy and motivation are low. We stay close to the floor the whole way through — settling into child's pose, opening the hips in a reclined butterfly, easing along the side body in banana pose, unwinding the spine with a reclined twist, and finally melting into a long savasana. Nothing here asks for strength or effort; we simply relax, breathe, and let gravity do the work.

## Who this class is for

Anyone feeling lazy, tired or unmotivated who still wants to give their body a little care. It's perfect for beginners and for anyone who wants a calming, low-effort practise to unwind with — at the end of a long day, before bed, or any time you need a gentle reset.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. A yoga block or cushion is nice to have nearby to support your hips or forehead in child's pose, but it's optional. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, so take whichever feels right and press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a couple of minutes or more; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — then come out slowly when it's time.
