## What this Yin class is

A gentle 33-minute Yin practice made for the end of the day — the kind of slow, quiet stretch that helps you set the day down before bed. We stay low to the floor the whole way through, starting in Corpse Pose to settle, then moving softly through Reclined Butterfly, a gentle hug for the lower back, Reclined Swan for the hips, Banana Pose along the side body, and a Reclined Twist for the spine, before resting again at the end. Each shape is held a while so gravity and your breath can do the work — and every pose has a lighter option and a deeper one, so you choose what feels right tonight.

## Who this class is for

Anyone winding down in the evening and looking for a soft, unhurried stretch before sleep. It's lovely after a long or busy day, when your body feels tight through the hips and lower back, or when your mind won't quite switch off and you'd like something calming to come home to. New to Yin? You're very welcome here — the shapes are simple and gentle, and I talk you into each one. Read the pose notes below first if you'd like to know what's coming.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props are required, though a cushion or folded blanket nearby is always welcome — a little support under the knees or hips can make the longer holds feel softer. Wear something comfortable, dim the lights if you can, and give yourself permission to pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your stretch — enough to feel it, but never forcing or straining. In a bedtime practice especially, softer is better.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a few minutes each; let the shape do the work while you breathe. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Soften your jaw, drop your shoulders, and rest your attention on your breath. Each time a thought drifts in, gently come back to the sensations you're feeling — that's how the body starts to let go.
