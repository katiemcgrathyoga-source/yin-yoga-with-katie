## What this Yin class is

A slow, gentle 26-minute morning practice — an easy way to greet the day and stretch out anything that settled in overnight. We begin softly in Child's Pose with a few side stretches, then wake the spine through Cat-Cow, open the hips in Baby Dragon and Twisted Dragon, take a full-body stretch in Downward Dog, and unwind the shoulders and neck before a quiet closing. Nothing here is rushed or demanding — just a warm, unhurried way to feel a little looser and more awake. Come as you are, and take the lighter or the deeper option in each shape as your body asks.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who likes to ease into the morning slowly rather than launch straight into something intense. It's lovely if you wake up feeling a little stiff, or you simply want a calm, gentle stretch to settle your mind before the day gets going. New to Yin? You're very welcome here — read the pose notes below first, then follow along and let the shapes meet you where you are.

## What you need

Just your mat and a little space to move. No props are required, though a cushion or folded blanket is always welcome under the hips or knees, and a couple of blocks or books under your hands can soften Dragon if you'd like a gentler version. Wear something comfy, and feel free to keep it warm and quiet around you.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real, honest stretch, but never forcing or straining, especially first thing in the morning.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a little while; let the shape do the work while you breathe. Come out slowly, and sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Soften your face and shoulders, and rest your attention on your breath — if the mind drifts, that's okay, just gently bring it back.
