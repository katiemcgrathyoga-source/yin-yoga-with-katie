## What this Yin class is

Seventy minutes of Yin to nourish your digestion. We move through shapes that stimulate the Liver, Gall Bladder, Kidney, Stomach and Spleen lines — opening the inner legs, lengthening the side body and adding gentle twists that compress and massage the belly. Everything is held for around three to five minutes, so you have real time to bring your attention into your digestive system and help things get moving again. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who wants to support their digestion and gut health, or who simply loves a slow, meditative practice that leaves the middle of the body feeling lighter. All levels are welcome. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still for a while. No props are required, though a couple of cushions or blocks nearby are lovely if you'd like a little support. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** We stay in each pose for three to five minutes; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath and the gentle sensations in your belly. When you come out, come out slowly.
