## What this Yin class is

A full hour of silent Yin — a deep full-body stretch with minimal cues and no props needed. I say just enough to guide you into each shape, then leave you to the quiet, so you can drop out of your head and fully into the pose. Long holds unwind the back, shoulders, hips and legs. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone who loves a peaceful, meditative practice with very little talking — and anyone who wants a deep stretch without gathering any props. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props required — though a cushion or blanket nearby is always welcome. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; the length is where the change happens. Come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — especially helpful in a silent practice.
