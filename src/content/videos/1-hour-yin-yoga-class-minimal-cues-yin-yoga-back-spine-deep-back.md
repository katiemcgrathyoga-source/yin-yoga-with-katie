## What this Yin class is

A full hour of quiet Yin for your back and spine. I give minimal cues — just enough to name each pose and how long we'll stay — then fall silent so you can drop out of your head and fully into the shape. We work gently through the whole length of the spine: soft back bends to open the front body, long forward folds to release the back, and a reclined twist to unwind it all. Held long and slow, these shapes are a lovely way to look after your spinal health. Come as you are — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone whose back feels tight, tired or compressed — after a long day at a desk, before bed, or any time you want to move quietly and care for your spine. It's a peaceful, meditative practice with very little talking, so it suits you if you'd rather stretch in silence than be guided the whole way through. Always respect your own body and your own limitations. New to Yin? Read the pose notes below first, then follow the shapes at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat, and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props are required — though a cushion, block or bolster nearby is always welcome to support your hips or head in the longer holds. Press pause whenever you need to.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a while; let the shape do the work, and come out sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Settle body and mind, and rest your attention on your breath — especially helpful in a silent practice.
