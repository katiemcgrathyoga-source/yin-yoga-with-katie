## What this Yin class is

A quiet 25-minute Yin practice for the neck and shoulders, held long and slow so tension has time to unwind. We open with a few soft neck stretches, then settle into three longer shapes — Thread-the-Needle, Puppy (or Melting Heart), and Eagle arms — each held for three to five minutes, with a rest in Child's Pose in between. It's a soothing, unhurried way to release the tightness that builds across the upper back and shoulders, and to help you calm the body and mind. Come as you are — every pose has a lighter option and a deeper one.

## Who this class is for

Anyone carrying stress or stiffness in the neck and shoulders — after a long day at a desk, before bed, or any time your upper body feels wound up and you'd like to soften it. The holds are long but gentle, and there's always a way to make each shape easier, so it's welcoming if you're newer to Yin as well. New to these poses? Read the pose notes below first, then follow along at your own pace.

## What you need

Just your mat and a warm, quiet space where you can be still. No props are required, though a cushion or folded blanket nearby is always welcome if you'd like a little padding under the knees or something soft to rest on. If a shape ever feels like too much, sit back into Child's Pose and rest — you're not doing anything wrong by coming out early.

## How Yin Yoga works — three simple cues

- **Find your edge.** Come to about 80% of your capacity — a real, honest stretch, but never forcing or straining.
- **Hold for time.** Yin poses are held a good while; stay slightly active, let the shape do the work, and come out slowly — sooner if you need to.
- **Be still.** Let the body settle and rest your attention on your breath, sending each inhale into wherever feels like it needs it most.
