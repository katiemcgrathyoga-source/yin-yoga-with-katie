hold_time: "3–5 minutes"
hold_seconds: 180
props: ["bolster", "block", "blanket"]
benefits:
  - "A soft, passive heart and lung opener — the prop does the work while you simply rest and receive the shape."
  - "Opens the chest and the front of the shoulders, encouraging fuller, easier breathing and countering a rounded upper back."
  - "Works the Heart and Lung lines across the chest and arms, with the Kidney, Urinary Bladder, Stomach and Spleen lines supporting the spine."
  - "Deeply calming. With the body held and nothing to hold, it is one of the kindest ways to melt open the front body — come as you are."
cues:
  - "Place a bolster or a block lengthways behind you, positioned to sit under your shoulder blades — not under the lower back or the neck."
  - "Lie back over it so the chest opens and drapes gently over the support, and let your head rest back. Add a folded blanket under the head if the neck feels strained."
  - "Let the arms fall open to the sides, palms up, so the shoulders and chest can spread."
  - "Choose your height: a low block or a bolster on its side for a gentle opening, a taller block on its edge for more. Every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one."
  - "Stretch the legs out long, or keep the knees bent with the soles down if your lower back prefers it."
  - "Find your edge — a real opening across the chest, never a strain. Then be still, breathe into the whole front body, and let the shape do the work."
transitions_in:
  - "Set the prop under the shoulder blades and lie back over it."
transitions_out:
  - "Come out slowly: bend the knees, roll gently to one side off the prop, and rest there for a breath or two before pressing up."
counterposes:
  - "A gentle reclined twist, or drawing the knees to the chest"
  - "Lying flat in Corpse for a few breaths to neutralise the spine"
cautions:
  - "Keep the support under the shoulder blades, not under the lower back — placed too low it can pinch the lumbar spine."
  - "Mind the neck: if the head hanging back feels strong, support it with a folded blanket so the throat stays comfortable."
  - "Approach cautiously with any neck or lower-back injury, and start with a lower prop height."
  - "Come out slowly and pause, as reclined openers can leave you a little lightheaded when you rise."
