props: ["cushion", "bolster", "blanket"]
benefits:
  - "A deep opener for the outer hips and glutes — that broad, satisfying stretch across the outside of the top thigh."
  - "Stimulates the gall bladder line down the outer leg and the urinary bladder line up the back."
  - "Folding forward adds a gentle release for the lower back and a quiet, inward turn for the mind."
  - "Helps undo the tightness that builds from long hours of sitting, walking and running."
cues:
  - "Come to sitting and stack your knees, one directly over the other, in front of your midline, with each foot drawn back beside the opposite hip."
  - "If the hips feel uneven or one sit bone lifts, slide a folded blanket under the higher hip so you feel level and grounded."
  - "Sit tall for a breath or two, feeling the outer hips. If that is already plenty, stay upright — that is the full pose."
  - "To go deeper, walk your hands forward and fold over the stacked legs on an out-breath, letting your spine round."
  - "Find your edge — about 80% of your capacity, a real stretch but never forcing. Rest your forehead or forearms on a bolster if the floor is far away."
  - "Be still and let the shape do the work. Then come out slowly and swap the cross of the legs for the second side."
transitions_in:
  - "From a seated position, cross one knee over the other and stack them, drawing the feet back beside the hips."
  - "From Deer or a cross-legged seat, lift and stack the knees one over the other."
transitions_out:
  - "Come out slowly. Rise back up through a rounded spine, then unstack the legs and extend them forward to release."
  - "Give the legs a gentle shake before setting up the second side."
counterposes:
  - "Extend both legs and lean back on your hands to let the hips settle."
  - "A reclined figure-four or a supine twist to soothe the outer hip."
  - "Rest in Corpse pose once both sides are complete."
cautions:
  - "This pose asks a lot of the knees. If the knees complain when you stack them, don't. A folded blanket between the thighs or under the hips eases the load — and if pain remains, come out."
  - "Keep the sensation in the outer hip, not the knee joint. Sharp or twisting knee sensation is your cue to back off."
  - "If stacking is too much for the hips, keep the bottom leg straight out in front (Folded Half Shoelace) for a gentler version."
  - "Let the spine round softly, but with disc trouble or sciatica, stay more upright and sit up on a cushion rather than folding deep."
