benefits:
  - "Gently lengthens the lower back and lets the whole spine settle after backbends or a busy day."
  - "Draws the hips back toward the heels, opening the hips and easing the knees into a soft fold."
  - "A grounding, inward pose that quiets the mind and slows the breath — a place to come home to at any point in your practice."
  - "Encourages steady breathing into the back body, which can feel wonderfully calming."
cues:
  - "Come to your hands and knees, then sink your hips back toward your heels."
  - "Take your knees as wide as feels good or keep them together — wide gives your belly more room, together feels more like a small parcel."
  - "Let your forehead rest on the mat, a block, or your stacked hands, and reach your arms forward, or let them trail back alongside your body."
  - "Find your edge — about 80% of your capacity, a real stretch but never forcing. Let the shape do the work and simply be still."
  - "If your hips float far from your heels, tuck a bolster or rolled blanket between your thighs and calves to rest on."
transitions_in:
  - "From hands and knees (Table), sit your hips back toward your heels and let your upper body fold down."
  - "From a seated position, come onto your knees and fold forward."
transitions_out:
  - "Come out slowly. Walk your hands back and rise up through the spine to sit on your heels."
  - "Pause and rest for a few breaths before moving on."
counterposes:
  - "A gentle seated or reclined twist to unwind the lower back."
  - "Sphinx or a soft, supported backbend to balance the forward fold."
cautions:
  - "If your knees complain, widen them, add padding behind them, or place a rolled blanket in the crease of the knees to ease the bend."
  - "Coming forward over the belly may be uncomfortable in later pregnancy — take the knees wide, or choose a different resting pose."
  - "If pressure builds in your head or you feel dizzy, turn your head to the side or rest your forehead higher on a block."
