hold_time: "3–5 minutes"
hold_seconds: 180
props: ["cushion", "bolster", "block", "blanket"]
benefits:
  - "Opens the inner thighs and adductors as the legs spread wide."
  - "Lengthens the hamstrings and the whole back line of the body as you fold forward."
  - "Gently stretches along the lower back and spine when you round softly toward the floor."
  - "A quietly opening, patient pose that rewards time and steady breathing over effort."
cues:
  - "Sit and open your legs wide into a comfortable V. Sit up onto a cushion so the pelvis can tilt forward more easily."
  - "Let your toes and knees roll up toward the ceiling rather than falling flat, so the knees stay safe."
  - "Walk your hands forward between your legs and fold from the hips, letting the spine round only gently as you settle."
  - "Find your edge — about 80% of your capacity, a real stretch but never forcing. Rest your forearms, or your head, on a bolster and let the shape do the work."
  - "You do not need to go far — even a small fold, or leaning to one leg at a time, gives plenty. Be still and let it deepen slowly."
transitions_in:
  - "From a seated position, open the legs wide, then walk the hands forward to fold."
  - "From Butterfly, extend the legs out wide into the straddle."
transitions_out:
  - "Come out slowly. Walk the hands back, rise up through the spine, then use your hands to draw the legs back together."
  - "Bounce or shake the legs gently, or lie back with the knees bent, to release the hips."
counterposes:
  - "Draw the knees together and hug them in, or lie back for a moment to release the inner thighs."
  - "A gentle seated twist to rebalance the lower back after the fold."
cautions:
  - "Protect the lower back — fold from the hips and round only gently, and sit up on a cushion so the pelvis can tip forward."
  - "Guard the inner knees and hamstrings by keeping a soft micro-bend in the knees and pointing the toes up toward the ceiling."
  - "If the hamstrings feel sharp or you have a hamstring injury, bend the knees more and rest the folded body on a bolster."
