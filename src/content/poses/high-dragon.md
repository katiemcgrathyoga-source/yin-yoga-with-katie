accessibility: "intermediate"
hold_time: "2–5 minutes per side"
props: ["folded blanket", "block"]
benefits:
  - "Lifting the torso tall lets your body weight sink straight down into the front of the back hip — a strong, direct hip-flexor opener."
  - "Releases the groin and the front of the back thigh as the pelvis descends."
  - "The upright chest adds a gentle opening across the front of the body and a lift through the heart."
  - "Stimulates the Stomach, Spleen, Liver and Gall Bladder meridian lines."
  - "A steady, dignified shape — more upright and awake than the folded Dragons."
cues:
  - "Begin in Dragon, right foot forward and left knee down, padding the back knee if you need it."
  - "Walk your hands back and rest them on the front thigh, then press down gently to lift the torso tall and upright."
  - "Let your body weight settle down through the pelvis so the hips sink toward the floor rather than the chest reaching up."
  - "Keep the front knee stacked over the ankle — check it stays above the heel, not pushing past the toes."
  - "Let the tailbone feel heavy and draw down; avoid arching sharply in the lower back. The lift comes from the whole spine, not a crease at the waist."
  - "Find your edge — about 80% of your capacity, a real stretch through the front of the back hip but never forcing."
  - "Soften your shoulders, be still, and let the shape do the work. Stay, then change sides."
transitions_in:
  - "From Dragon — walk the hands back onto the front thigh and lift the torso"
  - "From a standing low lunge — lower the back knee and rise tall"
transitions_out:
  - "Bring your hands back down to frame the front foot, then step back to tabletop or Downward-Facing Dog."
  - "Lower to Child's Pose to rest the hips."
counterposes:
  - "Child's Pose to release the lower back and hips"
  - "Downward-Facing Dog to lengthen and neutralise the legs"
cautions:
  - "Protect the front knee. Keep it stacked over the ankle and tracking with the toes — never let it push past the toes or fall inward as you sink."
  - "Support the back knee with a folded blanket; the upright weight loads it more directly here."
  - "Keep the tailbone heavy so the hip stretch doesn't turn into a pinch in the lower back. Ease the lift a little if the back complains."
  - "Come out slowly. Move gently into your counterpose and let the hips resettle before standing."
