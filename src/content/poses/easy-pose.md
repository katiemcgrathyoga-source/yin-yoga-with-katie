  - "A simple, comfortable seat for breathing and meditation, and a grounding way to begin or close a practice."
  - "Gently opens the hips and outer thighs as the knees ease toward the floor."
  - "Invites the spine to grow tall and the breath to move freely, which helps you feel calm and settled."
  - "Teaches the stillness at the heart of Yin — sitting quietly and simply being here."
cues:
  - "Sit cross-legged, letting each foot rest beneath the opposite knee, and settle onto your sitting bones."
  - "Sit up onto the edge of a cushion or folded blanket so the hips lift above the knees — this lets the spine rise easily."
  - "Rest your hands on your thighs or in your lap, soften your shoulders, and let the crown of your head float up."
  - "Come as you are. There is nothing to achieve here — simply breathe and be still."
  - "If the knees hang high or the outer hips feel tight, support each knee on a cushion or block so they can relax."
transitions_in:
  - "From standing or kneeling, come down to sit and cross the legs comfortably."
transitions_out:
  - "Come out slowly. Uncross the legs, extend them forward and give the knees a moment before you move on."
  - "Swap the cross of the legs from time to time so both sides share the work."
counterposes:
  - "Extend the legs long in Caterpillar or gently shake them out to release the hips."
  - "A soft twist to free the spine after sitting."
cautions:
  - "If the knees feel any strain, raise your seat higher and support the knees on cushions so they are not left hanging."
  - "Sitting on the floor may be hard on the lower back for some — lift the hips more, or sit against a wall for support."
  - "Change the crossing of the legs regularly so one hip is not always working harder than the other."
