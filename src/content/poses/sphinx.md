accessibility: "beginner"
hold_time: "Sphinx: up to 5 minutes. Seal (straight arms): start with 1-minute rounds, rest, and repeat."
props: ["bolster", "blanket"]
benefits:
  - "A gentle-to-deep backbend that compresses and stimulates the sacral-lumbar arch — the lower-back curve that long hours of sitting tend to flatten."
  - "Tones the spine; often therapeutic for bulging or herniated discs, since it's a spinal extension rather than a flexion."
  - "Stimulates the kidneys and adrenal glands through gentle compression."
  - "Works the Urinary Bladder and Kidney lines along with the Stomach and Spleen meridians."
cues:
  - "Lie on your belly and prop yourself onto your forearms, elbows just ahead of your shoulders. This is Sphinx."
  - "Notice your lower back. If the sensation is too strong, slide the elbows further forward to lower the chest; if you want more, draw them back under you."
  - "Let the legs be heavy. Keeping them apart deepens the low-back sensation; together spreads it more evenly along the spine."
  - "Relax the buttocks and shoulders, and breathe down into the belly."
  - "For the deeper Seal, straighten the arms with the hands slightly forward of the shoulders and turned out a little, and let the spine hang into the arch."
  - "Hold Sphinx up to 5 minutes. For Seal, work in shorter one-minute rounds with rests between."
transitions_in:
  - "From lying on your belly, prop up onto the forearms"
transitions_out:
  - "Slowly lower the chest to the floor, turn the head to one side, and rest. Sliding one knee up can ease the lower back."
counterposes:
  - "Child's Pose, moved into slowly"
  - "A few gentle, breath-paced Cat/Cow rounds (not your deepest)"
cautions:
  - "Ease off — or come out — at any sharp sensation in the lower back. This is a backbend, and sharp pain is a stop signal."
  - "Approach cautiously with a sensitive lower back or tight sacrum: rest on the ribs with the elbows slid forward, or simply lie on the belly."
  - "If pregnant, avoid pressing the belly into the floor — support the pelvis and forearms with bolsters (the supported version is generally considered safe)."
  - "Skip it if you have a headache."
