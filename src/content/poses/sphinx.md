benefits:
  - "A gentle backbend that restores the natural curve of the lower back — the very curve that long hours of sitting tend to flatten."
  - "Compresses and stimulates the sacral-lumbar area, tonifying the spine and often feeling therapeutic for a tired lower back."
  - "Stimulates the kidneys and adrenals through soft compression, working the Kidney and Urinary Bladder lines along with the Stomach and Spleen meridians."
  - "A calm, accessible way into backbending — every pose has a gentler option and a deeper one, and Sphinx is the gentle door into this family."
cues:
  - "Lie on your belly and prop yourself up onto your forearms, elbows a little ahead of your shoulders."
  - "Notice your lower back. If the sensation feels too strong, slide the elbows further forward to lower the chest; if you want more, draw them back beneath you."
  - "Let the legs be heavy and soft. Keeping them apart deepens the low-back sensation; drawing them together spreads it more evenly along the spine."
  - "Relax the buttocks, soften the shoulders down away from the ears, and breathe down into the belly."
  - "Find your edge — about 80%, a clear stretch through the front of the body but never forcing. Then let the shape do the work and be still."
transitions_in:
  - "From lying on your belly, prop up onto the forearms."
transitions_out:
  - "Come out slowly: lower the chest to the floor and turn the head to one side to rest. Sliding one knee up towards the hip can ease the lower back."
counterposes:
  - "Child's Pose, moved into slowly"
  - "A few gentle, breath-paced rounds of arching and rounding the back (not your deepest)"
cautions:
  - "Ease off — or come out — at any sharp sensation in the lower back. This is a backbend, and sharp pain is a stop signal."
  - "Approach cautiously with a sensitive lower back or tight sacrum: rest lower on the ribs with the elbows slid forward, or simply lie on the belly."
  - "If pregnant, avoid pressing the belly into the floor — support the pelvis and forearms with bolsters."
  - "Skip it if you have a headache."
