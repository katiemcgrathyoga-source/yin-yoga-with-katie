accessibility: "intermediate"
hold_time: "2–5 minutes per side"
props: ["bolster", "block", "folded blanket"]
benefits:
  - "Deepens the groin and hip-flexor opening of Dragon by bringing the upper body closer to the floor."
  - "Drawing the front foot wider adds a strong release through the inner thigh and groin."
  - "Lengthens the front of the back hip and thigh as the pelvis settles lower."
  - "Stimulates the Stomach, Spleen, Liver and Gall Bladder meridian lines."
  - "A deep, surrendering shape that invites the hips to let go a little more with every breath."
cues:
  - "Begin in Dragon, right foot forward and left knee down, padding the back knee if you need it."
  - "Walk your hands forward and lower onto your forearms, resting them on the floor, a bolster, or a block set inside the front foot."
  - "If it feels available, let the front foot ease out toward the edge of the mat and roll onto its outer edge — this opens the inner thigh and groin further."
  - "Keep the front knee stacked over the ankle even as it tracks outward, and never let it push past the toes."
  - "Let the hips be heavy and sink toward the floor. Find your edge — about 80% of your capacity, a real stretch but never forcing."
  - "Rest your head or let it hang softly. Be still, and let the shape do the work."
  - "Stay, then press up and change sides."
transitions_in:
  - "From Dragon — walk the hands forward and lower to the forearms or a block"
  - "From tabletop — step one foot forward and settle down onto the forearms"
transitions_out:
  - "Press your hands into the floor to lift the torso back up to Dragon or tabletop."
  - "Step the front foot back and lower to Child's Pose to rest."
counterposes:
  - "Child's Pose to release the hips and lower back"
  - "Downward-Facing Dog to lengthen and neutralise the legs"
cautions:
  - "Protect the front knee. Keep it stacked over the ankle and tracking with the toes — never let it drift past the toes or collapse inward, even as the foot moves wider."
  - "Support the back knee with a folded blanket; the lower the hips sink, the more weight the back knee carries."
  - "The groin stretch here can build quickly. Come down onto a bolster or block rather than the floor if it feels too strong, and back off at the first sharp sensation in the inner thigh."
  - "Come out slowly. The hip flexors can feel vulnerable straight after — move gently into your counterpose before standing."
