benefits:
  - "A deep hip opener that lets gravity, rather than muscular effort, draw the front hip into strong external rotation."
  - "Lengthens the hip flexors and quadriceps of the long back leg."
  - "Folding forward gives a long, calming release to the lower back."
  - "Stimulates the Liver, Gall Bladder, Kidney, Stomach and Spleen meridian lines."
  - "A grounding, surrendering shape that settles the nervous system — well suited to a slow, quiet practice."
cues:
  - "Come as you are. Begin on your hands and knees in a neutral tabletop, or step back from Swan."
  - "Slide your right knee forward toward your right wrist and let the shin angle across the mat. The more diagonal the shin, the gentler it is on the knee — let your hips decide the angle."
  - "Flex the right foot to support the knee, and slide your left leg long behind you, the top of the foot on the floor."
  - "Before you settle, check the front knee. If you feel any pinch, draw the front foot in closer toward your right hip until the knee goes quiet."
  - "On an exhale, walk your hands forward and lower your chest over a few breaths — down onto your forearms, a bolster, a stack of blankets, or the mat."
  - "Let your forehead rest and your whole body grow heavy. Find your edge — about 80% of your capacity, a real stretch but never forcing."
  - "Soften completely. Be still, and let the shape do the work. Stay, then repeat on the second side."
transitions_in:
  - "From Swan — walk the hands forward and lower the chest"
  - "From tabletop or Cat (hands and knees)"
  - "From Downward-Facing Dog — draw one knee forward between the hands"
transitions_out:
  - "Press the floor away with your hands and slowly rise back up through Swan."
  - "Tuck the back toes, press back into Child's Pose to rest."
counterposes:
  - "Child's Pose to release and rebalance the hips"
  - "Windshield wipers, lying or seated, to internally rotate the hips back to neutral"
cautions:
  - "Knees first. This pose can drive pressure into the front knee, particularly when the hips are tight. Keep the front foot flexed and, if you feel any pinch, bring the foot in toward the hip until the knee is comfortable. Approach with great caution, or skip, if you have a meniscus injury or any knee instability."
  - "If the hips don't open easily, slip a folded blanket under the seat of the bent-knee side so the pelvis stays level and weight doesn't dump into the knee."
  - "If folding forward compresses the lower back, stay higher on a bolster or forearms rather than reaching for the floor."
  - "Come out slowly. After a long hold the joints feel fragile for a moment — that passes; move gently into your counterpose."
faq:
