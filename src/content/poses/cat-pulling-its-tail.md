benefits:
  - "Takes the wind-down of a reclined twist and adds a lovely stretch through the front of the thigh and hip flexor."
  - "Releases the lower back and outer hip while gently opening the quad of the extended leg."
  - "Wrings the spine and massages the belly, which can ease digestion."
  - "Opens the chest and shoulder as the top arm reaches back to catch the foot."
  - "A satisfying two-in-one shape — twist and front-line opener in a single pose."
cues:
  - "Begin lying on your back, then let both knees drop over to your left."
  - "Slide the bottom (left) leg long down the mat, keeping the top (right) knee bent and resting toward the floor."
  - "Reach back with your right hand and catch the left foot or ankle — loop a strap around it if it's out of reach."
  - "Draw the foot gently toward you until you feel the front of that lower thigh open up."
  - "Open your left arm out to the side and turn your gaze softly that way."
  - "Find your edge — about 80% of your capacity, a real stretch but never forcing — then be still and let the shape do the work."
  - "If the twist bites in the lower back, tuck a cushion under the top knee to take the strain."
transitions_in:
  - "From a Reclined Twist with knees dropped to one side, extend the bottom leg long and reach the top hand back for the bottom foot."
transitions_out:
  - "Come out slowly — release the foot, draw the knees back to centre, and rest on your back before changing sides."
counterposes:
  - "Lie on your back with knees bent and let the spine and hips re-centre."
  - "A gentle knees-to-chest hug to settle the lower back."
cautions:
  - "Ease off if you have disc trouble — keep the twist gentle and let a cushion under the knee take the strain rather than deepening the rotation."
  - "Go easy on the knee of the extended leg; hold the ankle or use a strap rather than cranking the foot toward the hip."
  - "Stop pulling on the foot if you feel anything sharp in the knee or lower back."
