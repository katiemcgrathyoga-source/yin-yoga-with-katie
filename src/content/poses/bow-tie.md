benefits:
  - "Lets the weight of your own body open the shoulders and the space between the shoulder blades — no effort required."
  - "Releases the upper back and the backs of the shoulders, easing rounded-forward posture."
  - "Draws the shoulder blades apart, stretching the small and large intestine lines across the upper back."
  - "Deeply restful — once you've settled, there is nothing to do but breathe."
cues:
  - "Lie face down and cross your arms underneath your chest, one forearm over the other, so the elbows point out to the sides like a bow tie."
  - "Rest your forehead on the mat, or turn your head to one side, or set your chin down — whatever your neck prefers."
  - "Let your chest sink toward the floor so the weight of your torso opens the shoulders."
  - "The higher the arms are stacked toward the collarbones, the stronger the stretch — start lower and adjust."
  - "Find your edge — about 80% of your capacity, a real stretch but never forcing — then let the shape do the work."
  - "Swap which arm is on top halfway through, as the two arrangements feel quite different."
  - "Cushion the forehead or the chest if you need a little more comfort."
transitions_in:
  - "From lying face down, cross the forearms beneath the chest and settle the torso onto them."
transitions_out:
  - "Come out slowly — uncross the arms, bring the hands beside the chest, and press gently back to rest in Child's Pose."
counterposes:
  - "Child's Pose to round and release the upper back."
  - "A slow shoulder roll or gentle arm circles to bring the shoulders back to neutral."
cautions:
  - "Go gently with any shoulder injury — this is a real load on the shoulder joint, so keep within a comfortable range."
  - "If you feel any tingling or numbness down the arms, ease the arms lower or come out; the crossed position can compress the nerves."
  - "Support the forehead or turn the head only as far as the neck is happy to go."
