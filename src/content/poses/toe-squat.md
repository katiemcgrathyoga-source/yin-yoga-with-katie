  - "Gives a strong, direct stretch to the soles of the feet and the undersides of the toes — areas that rarely get any attention."
  - "Opens the toe joints and stimulates the many meridian lines that begin and end in the feet."
  - "Can leave the feet feeling lighter and more awake, which many people love for standing and walking."
  - "A brief, focused pose that teaches you to breathe steadily through intensity."
cues:
  - "Come to kneeling, then tuck all ten toes under, including the little toes, so the toe pads press into the mat."
  - "Slowly sit your hips back toward your heels, letting your weight settle onto the balls of the feet and the toes."
  - "Rest your hands on your thighs and lengthen up through the spine as the feet do their work."
  - "This one is intense — find your edge and no further. You may only need a short hold, and that is completely fine."
  - "If the little toes will not tuck under, help them along with your fingers, or ease some weight forward onto your hands."
transitions_in:
  - "From kneeling or Table, tuck the toes under and sit the hips back toward the heels."
transitions_out:
  - "Come out slowly. Lift the hips, untuck the toes, and sit back on the tops of the feet — or move straight into Ankle Stretch as its counter."
  - "You may like to shake the feet out or circle the ankles afterwards."
counterposes:
  - "Ankle Stretch, sitting back on the untucked feet, is the natural counter to Toe Squat."
  - "Child's Pose or a few gentle ankle circles to release the feet."
cautions:
  - "This is intense on the toes and feet — keep the hold short and come out at the first sign of cramp."
  - "A rolled blanket tucked under the ankles eases the pressure and lets you stay a little longer in comfort."
  - "If you have injured toes or acute foot problems, skip this one or lighten the load by keeping weight in your hands."

    a: "Toe Squat mainly works the feet, toes and ankles. Gives a strong, direct stretch to the soles of the feet and the undersides of the toes — areas that rarely get any attention."

    a: "You don't have to, but a blanket or block can make Toe Squat more comfortable and help you relax into the hold. Props in Yin aren't a shortcut — they let you stay longer and soften more deeply."
