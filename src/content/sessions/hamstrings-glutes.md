why: >
  Your hamstrings do the hard eccentric braking in every stride, and they're one of
  the most commonly strained muscles in runners. When the glutes are tight or
  sluggish, the hamstrings take on even more. Giving both back their length keeps
  that load shared, not dumped on one muscle.
angle: "Your hamstrings do the hardest work in every stride — give them their length back."
intro: >
  A slow release for the whole back of the legs and the glutes. Fold gently, let the
  breath soften you, and give the hamstrings the unhurried time they never get.
steps:
  - pose: "caterpillar"
    seconds: 180
    sides: 1
    note: "Legs long, fold down over them. A long, calming stretch down the whole back body."
  - pose: "half-butterfly"
    seconds: 180
    sides: 2
    note: "One leg out, one folded in. Fold over the straight leg to reach that hamstring on its own."
  - pose: "sleeping-swan"
    seconds: 120
    sides: 2
    note: "Front shin across the mat, fold forward. Releases the glute and deep outer hip."
  - pose: "reclined-hamstring"
    seconds: 60
    sides: 2
    note: "On your back, one leg up with a strap around the foot. Gentle, supported hamstring length."
scale:
  - level: "New"
    note: "Bend the knees generously — folding from the hips matters far more than reaching your feet. Use a strap for the reclined hold."
  - level: "Returning"
    note: "Ease a little deeper and let the head hang heavy. Stay the full three minutes in Caterpillar."
  - level: "Experienced"
    note: "Add a minute to the folds, softening the knees only as much as you need — let gravity do the rest."
