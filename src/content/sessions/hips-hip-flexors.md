why: >
  Running flexes the hip thousands of times, and most runners also sit at a desk
  all day — together that shortens the hip flexors. Tight hips shorten your stride,
  tip the pelvis forward (loading the low back) and stop the glutes doing their
  job. A few quiet minutes here after a run keeps the hip open before the tightness
  settles in.
angle: "Running plus sitting equals tight hips — open them first, and your whole stride frees up."
intro: >
  A short one for right after a run, while you're still warm. We'll open the front
  of the hips, release the glutes for balance, and finish with a gentle twist.
  Nothing forced — just settle in and let the holds do the work.
steps:
  - pose: "sphinx"
    seconds: 120
    sides: 1
    note: "Onto your forearms. A soft, passive lift — open the whole front of the hips."
  - pose: "dragon"
    seconds: 120
    sides: 2
    note: "Deep low lunge. Sink the hips forward and down — the big hip-flexor opener. Cushion under the back knee."
  - pose: "sleeping-swan"
    seconds: 120
    sides: 2
    note: "Front shin across the mat, fold forward. Releases the glute and outer hip — the balance to all that forward motion."
  - pose: "reclined-twist"
    seconds: 60
    sides: 2
    note: "On your back, drop the knees to one side. A gentle wring-out to finish and settle."
scale:
  - level: "New"
    note: "More support, less depth. Cushion under the back knee in Dragon; stay tall in Sleeping Swan rather than folding. Two minutes is plenty — come out sooner if you need to."
  - level: "Returning"
    note: "Meet your edge. Sink a little deeper, fold forward in Sleeping Swan, and let the breath do the softening."
  - level: "Experienced"
    note: "Stay longer. Add a minute to each hold, and notice the deeper release in the second half — that's the connective tissue letting go."
when:
  - "Right after a run — the ideal window, while you're warm."
  - "Any evening your hips feel tight from sitting."
  - "As the hip block inside a longer Full Reset session."
