why: >
  Your calves and feet store and return huge amounts of energy with every step, and
  they stiffen with mileage. Stiff ankles — limited dorsiflexion — are linked to
  altered mechanics and higher injury risk, because the force has to go somewhere,
  usually up into the knee and hip. Almost no runner works here, which is exactly
  why it makes such a difference.
angle: "It all starts at your feet — stiff ankles change everything above them."
intro: >
  The bit everyone forgets. We'll wake up the soles and toes, free the ankles both
  ways, and lengthen the calves — the foundation the rest of your stride sits on.
steps:
  - pose: "toe-squat"
    seconds: 120
    sides: 1
    note: "Kneel with the toes tucked under — a strong stretch through the soles and toes. Ease in; 30–60 seconds is plenty at first, hands down to take off weight."
  - pose: "ankle-stretch"
    seconds: 120
    sides: 1
    note: "Tops of the feet down, sit back gently — the opposite stretch, along the shins and fronts of the ankles."
  - pose: "squat"
    seconds: 180
    sides: 1
    note: "A deep, supported squat — opens the ankles, calves and hips at once. Heels on a rolled blanket if they lift."
  - pose: "dangling"
    seconds: 120
    sides: 1
    note: "Standing forward fold, soft knees, heavy head — lengthens the calves and the whole back line."
  - pose: "saddle"
    seconds: 120
    sides: 1
    note: "Optional half-saddle, one leg at a time — reaches the shins, ankles and quads. Prop behind you; skip if the knees complain."
scale:
  - level: "New"
    note: "Keep the toe and ankle stretches short and light, hands taking weight. Sit on a block in the squat."
  - level: "Returning"
    note: "Stay a little longer in the squat and let the heels settle toward the floor."
  - level: "Experienced"
    note: "Build the toe-squat toward two full minutes, and add the half-saddle for the shins."
when:
  - "After a run, especially on hard roads or trails."
  - "Any time your feet, ankles or calves feel stiff or achy."
  - "First thing, to free the ankles before a day on your feet."
