If your mind is still racing when your head hits the pillow, a short, slow Yin practice in the hour before bed can be a lovely way to change gears. Yin Yoga asks almost nothing of you — you settle into a handful of floor-based shapes, hold each one for a few quiet minutes, and let gravity do the work. The long holds and slow, unhurried breathing gently invite the body out of "doing" mode and toward its rest state.

This is a **three-night wind-down** — the first three evenings of my Bedtime Yin series. Each is a complete 30-minute class, and they're meant to be done **in order**, one per night. Roll out your mat somewhere warm and dim, and follow along.

## How do you use Yin Yoga before bed?

Keep it simple and comfortable: practise in loose, warm clothes, dim the lights, and have a cushion or folded blanket nearby to sit or rest on. Nothing here is a stretch you push into — you're looking for a mild, tolerable sensation you can breathe with, then stillness. If a shape is too much, come out sooner or take the gentler option. Little and often does more than long and rare.

## Night 1 — settle and soften

Start with **[Night 1: a 30-minute full-body wind-down](/videos/yin-yoga-for-sleep-challenge-day-1)**. It opens gently and works evenly through the whole body, so it's a kind introduction if you're new to Yin. You'll move through **[Butterfly](/poses/butterfly)** to open the hips, **[Sphinx](/poses/sphinx)** for a soft, propped-up backbend, **[Child's Pose](/poses/childs-pose)** to fold in and quiet down, and **[Eagle](/poses/eagle)** arms to release between the shoulder blades where the day tends to collect.

## Night 2 — open the front of the body

**[Night 2: a full-body evening stretch](/videos/30-min-yin-yoga-for-sleep-full-body-evening-stretch-day-2-bedtime)** leans into the chest and back body. **[Melting Heart](/poses/melting-heart)** opens the chest and shoulders after a day hunched over a screen; **[Caterpillar](/poses/caterpillar)** is a long, calming forward fold down the back of the body; and a reclined **[Reclined Swan](/poses/reclined-swan)** eases the outer hips while you're already lying down — nearly asleep, ideally. **[Child's Pose](/poses/childs-pose)** returns as a resting point.

## Night 3 — release the hips and shoulders

By **[Night 3: bedtime Yin for hips and shoulders](/videos/30-min-bedtime-yin-yoga-for-hips-shoulders-challenge-day-3)**, you're releasing the two places most of us hold tension. **[Dragon](/poses/dragon)** opens the front of the hips and the hip flexors that tighten from sitting; a simple cross-legged **[Easy Pose](/poses/easy-pose)** fold grounds you; and **[Cow Face](/poses/cow-face)** arms unwind the shoulders. End however you like to rest — flat on your back, or curled on your side.

## What if I want to keep going?

Three nights is enough to feel the difference — most people notice they get to bed a little more settled. If you'd like to carry it on, the whole thing is laid out for you as a guided path below, so you never have to decide what to practise. Come back to any night whenever the day has been a lot.

*This is a gentle movement practice, not medical advice. If you're pregnant or have a back, hip or knee concern, take the gentler option in each shape and check with your doctor if you're unsure.*
