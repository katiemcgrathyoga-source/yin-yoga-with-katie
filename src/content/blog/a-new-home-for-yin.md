For a long time now, everything I make has lived on YouTube. I love YouTube — it's where we found each other — but it was never built for the way people actually *practise* Yin. You'd come looking for "something for my hips before bed," and end up scrolling past thumbnails, half-remembering which class was the good one, talking yourself out of rolling out the mat at all.

So I made something better. This whole site is a proper home for the practice — free, no login, nothing to buy. Just the calmest, clearest way I could imagine to find the right practice and actually do it. Here's what's waiting for you.

## A library that finally makes sense

Every class I've made, in one place — and you can filter it the way your body actually asks for things. *Under 20 minutes. For sleep. Hips and lower back. Full body.* Tap what's true for you tonight and the right classes rise to the top. No more scrolling, no more guessing.

[Browse the library →](/)

## A pose library, to understand the shapes

Not just *what* to do, but *why*. Each Yin pose now has its own page — what it targets, how long to hold, the gentler option and the deeper one, and the little cues that make all the difference. And every class links straight to the poses inside it, so if you've ever wondered "wait, what's this one doing for me?", the answer is one tap away.

[Explore the poses →](/poses)

## Guided programs, so you never have to decide

This is the one I'm most excited about. If you've ever wanted a *path* rather than a pick-and-mix, I've built short guided programs — a few classes each — that take the deciding off your plate. **The Bedtime Reset. Free Your Hips. Full-Body Flexibility. The Calm Reset.** Press play on day one and just follow along.

[See the programs →](/programs)

## Build your own month — with reminders

There's a calendar planner where you can lay out your practice, drop classes (or a whole program) onto the days that suit you, and send the lot to your own Google, Apple or Outlook calendar — reminders and all. Because the practice that changes you is the one you actually come back to.

[Plan your practice →](/calendar)

## And a gift to begin with

If you're new here — or you just need permission to stop for a couple of hours — I've made you a **free two-hour retreat**: a guided journey through Yin, restorative yoga and Yoga Nidra to melt off a hard week and settle your nervous system. It's normally a members-only class, and it's yours to keep.

[Claim your free retreat →](/free-class)
